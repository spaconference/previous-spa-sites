SPA Conference session: UML Use Case Workshop

One-line description:A practical UML approach to domain modelling, actors discovery, development of use cases, and to transition to design.
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Working group [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:This workshop is specifically designed to develop the skills necessary to generate use cases that capture and express requirements robustly, effectively and unambiguously using the Unified Modeling Language (UML).

It focuses on the key concepts of domain modelling, actors, use cases and stereotyped-dependencies as well as scenarios, sequence diagrams, statecharts and activity diagrams.

The workshop reinforces the role of use case and behavioural specification as a means of improved communication across domains and teams.
 
Audience background:The session will be valuable for all those who involved in the development or receipt of requirements.
Familiarity with software requirements and the development of software systems will be beneficial. UML is not essential.
 
Benefits of participating:Understand how system behaviour may be described using use cases.
Appreciate techniques for defining the system boundary, identifying actors and discovering use cases use-cases.
Understand the role and benefits of domain modelling.
Understand methods for documenting use cases textually or using elements of UML and providing trace ability to requirements.
 
Materials provided:Slides for presentation.
Guide to writing use cases.
 
Process:Although lecturer lead, the session is interactive and discussion is encouraged. This is a rapid pace and could if required be extended to 150 minutes with greater involvement.

0-5 Introduction to use cases
5 -15 Domain modelling
15-20 Actors and the system boundary
25-35 Discovering use cases
35-40 Advanced use case notation
45-50 UML diagrams in support of use cases
55 70 Use case analysis
70-75 Questions & Discussion
 
Outputs:Summary of presented slides and a sharing of concepts and ideas
 
History:This is a condensed variation of a 2 day interactive workshop that has been delivered by Objektum Ltd to clients in various sectors with considerable success and benefit to the client.
