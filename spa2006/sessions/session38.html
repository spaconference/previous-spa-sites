SPA Conference session: Thinking for a Change

One-line description:Applying the Theory of Constraints' Thinking Processes
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Workshop [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Objectives

The objective of this session is to introduce participants to the "Thinking Processes" of the Theory of Constraints and to let them practise by applying these problem solving tools to agile software development.

Contents

This session gives an interactive introduction to the "Thinking Processes" of the Theory of Constraints. The Thinking Tools provide a set of processes, techniques and diagrams to analyze:
* where we are
* where we want to go
* what's keeping us from getting there
* how to resolve conflicts

We will explore the following tools:
* the ''Current Reality Tree'' describes the current system, to clarify and reach consensus about where we are.
* the ''Future Reality Tree'' describes the situation as we would like it to be
* the ''Negative Branch Reservations'' allow us describe the obstacles that hinder us in reaching the desired future reality and negative effects that may arise from the future reality. These are the obstacles we need to overcome to make the change.
* the ''Evaporating cloud'' allows us to examine conflicts and the assumptions underlying them, so that we can resolve the conflict.

The tools will be applied to situations involving the transition to Agile processes. These analyses will therefore be recognizable and relevant to the participants. The participants can contribute their experiences to enrich the core problem.
 
Audience background:team leads, coaches, project managers (experience level: journeyman / master)
 
Benefits of participating:This session is for people who want understand why things are the way they are and how to bring about changes more effectively.

The participants will learn a number of new problem solving tools and learn new ways to look at (IT) projects.
