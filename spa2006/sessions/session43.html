SPA Conference session: Going Offshore -Benefits and Perils Explored

One-line description:An investigation into the benefits, pitfalls and emotional turmoil associated with commissioning offshore companies to undertake software delivery.
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Goldfish bowl [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:The session will allow participants to explore the pros and cons of one of today's hot topics - the use of offshore developers to deliver software products. The session will be lead by Rob Day (who helped an Indian software house set up an object development centre in Mumbai) and Genny Dixon from e-Skills, the IT sector skills council. e-Skills have in depth knowledge of the skill shortfall facing the UK software industry and are playing a major role in upskilling the UK labour force to address that shortfall.

A panel of invited participants will seed the session. These will include:
John Brightwell - Euro-soft (outsourcing to Russia)
Ian Soden - LBI-Linkhand (outsourcing to India)
Stephen Hutchinson - independent (victim)
Tom Owens - DSDM white paper author (others invited)

The DSDM team are considering using the insights gained in preparing the white paper to author a book on the subject.
 
Audience background:Audience is likely to be mixed:
- managers who have done it and want to share experiences (good and bad)
- managers who need to consider it as an option (perhaps unwillingly)
- developers who have been involved
- developers who have suffered as a consequence
 
Benefits of participating:With a degree of structure and facilitation the debate will get past the raw emotion stage and allow an objective consideration of a modern day reality of software practice.
 
Materials provided:Mindmap summary from previous session.
Precis of reference materials on the topic (for example the thinking behind the recent DSDM white paper on the topic).
 
Process:This session will follow classic goldfish bowl format with mid-session break
 
Outputs:Summary of findings/conclusions -- posters and web follow up
 
History:Topic was aired in Dyna-BOF session (2004)
