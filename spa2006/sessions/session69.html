SPA Conference session: Evolution and recycling of software techniques

One-line description:Work out what underlies current ideas, techniques and standards - their evolution from earlier ideas in different contexts
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Workshop [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Objectives
* to document in a structured manner the evolution of some important current ideas in software engineering
* to explain what old farts mean when they say "there's nothing new" or "everything used to be simpler and better"
Content
* the leaders will provide a process to facilitate the achievement of the objectives; see the other sections for details
 
Audience background:* anyone who likes to think about underlying principles and the evolution of ideas
* old farts who have seen it all (many times)
 
Benefits of participating:* see a range of key ideas and understand the elements in their evolution
* think hard about just what the basics are
 
Materials provided:Workshop process plan
Notation for capturing evolution
 
Process:Introduction [2m]
Brainstorm key topics [5m]
* used to extend/alter a pre-prepared list
* choose topics to begin with (one per group plus one)
Set up working groups [3m]
* choose topic to work on
* equalise the groups
Live collaborative process demo [10m]
* quick explanation of notation
* everyone joins in the analysis of one of the topics
Groups work #1 [25m]
* Prepare interim posters [5m]
= tidied up version of the evolution charts
Mutual review to improve the posters [15m]
Create snappy phrases [5m]
Reflection, feedback [5m]
=================================
Examples
* services from components from objects
* XP from RAD from JFDI
* XML from abstract syntax
* Java becoming commercially acceptable (and even Smalltalk before it)
Requests
* better ideas for the title
* offers to co-lead
* any suggestions for technique
* session type - sofa (too unstructured), 150m workshop (get deeper)
 
Outputs:* a poster listing the ideas we considered and how they evolved
* list of snappy phrases that anyone can use to impress others
* web page on SPA website
 
History:* none as a conference session - thought and discussed the topics many times
