SPA Conference session: Early Aspects

One-line description:Early Aspects – why, what and how.
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Hot topic [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Early Aspects pushes the focus of thinking about crosscutting concerns (Aspects) earlier in the software lifecycle into the Requirements Management (RM) and Architecture Definition activities.

This introduction covers the motivation behind Early Aspects, what is involved and current approaches.
 
Audience background:Audience members should have an understanding of crosscutting concerns e.g. through programming using AOP. The presentation will be balanced between RM and Architecture.
 
Benefits of participating:Audience members will get early visibility of an emerging field, some idea of the state of the art and some indication of whether they should look into the area further.
 
Materials provided:Presentation slides (which will include links for further information)
 
Process:Timetable
- 00:00 - 00:15 - Slides: Early Aspects – Why, What and How?
- 00:15 - 00:25 – Facilitated group discussion

The discussion could be seeded with some of the following questions, for example,:

- How would Early Aspects fit into an Agile approach – if at all?
- Do people see this as any use to them – now or ever? Why?
- Should people start with Aspect-Oriented Programming first?
