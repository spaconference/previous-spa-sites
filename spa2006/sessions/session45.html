SPA Conference session: Modelling with views

One-line description:Rather than break a big model into components, you could present the model as a number of views - the session will present one way to combine several views of a big model.
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Case study [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:To take a simple example, you could view the happening of someone renting a video as a change in a point-of-sale world (something to be paid for was added to an ongoing sale) or as a change in the rental world (a new rental agreement was made). You could model from other points of view, too, such as stock management and customer handling.

You could try to model all these views at once. Or you could model them separately, and later combine them. How might you combine the business rules concerning adding a sale item to a sale with those concerning making a new rental agreement? Modellers working for InferData are often able to use a disarmingly simple answer: logical-and. (For those who know Catalysis, the approach uses the idea of effects.)

The session will present a simplified case study (video rentals), built on ideas arising in modelling work carried out for several clients. The presenter's goals for the session are:

- to show you a way to combine views of a single happening

- to show how this can be done even in very precise models

- to explore with you where best to exploit the technique.
 
Audience background:- Should have experience of building UML models

- Should be prepared to read precise models (although detailed knowledge of OCL is not necessary)

- Should be willing to explore not-quite-standard-UML ideas

- Should be interested in learning an extra interesting way of thinking
 
Benefits of participating:- Learn a novel way to divide a large modelling problem into parts

- Gain experience of models having the level of precision needed for MDA
 
Materials provided:- A set of slides presenting the case study

- Access (later) to a first draft of a full-length book describing the case study
 
Process:Mostly taught, but with audience participation welcomed throughout and actively managed in the discussion phase.
