SPA Conference session: New Media Web Developers and the Significance of Flash ActionScipt

One-line description:Pick up some useful insights into the use of Macromedia's Flash ActionScript.
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Case study [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Flash ActionScript is the object-oriented language that underpins the development of interactive "movies" for use via web browsers and has emerged as a key tool in the rapid delivery of effective e-Learning solutions.

The objective of this session is to provide participants with insights into the use of this technology.

The content will be structured around the following topics:
- the evolution of the language in the context of the need for increased sophistication in the toolset, including use of XML
- the use of UML "lite" as a means of exploiting the language's component development potential in support of the intensely iterative development cycle
- an agile audit (what worked; what didn't; what was missed; and why)
- avoiding our pitfalls
- the Flash community (or how graphic designers became software developers)
- future Flash (where the technology might go following Adobe's acquisition of Macromedia).
 
Audience background:No experience of Flash required. We will show you some ActionScript and several UML diagrams.
 
Benefits of participating:Increased awareness of how an OO language and its community of users have evolved largely outside the mainstream of software development. Participants will also see how a small organisation has been able to benefit from the application of UML and agile techniques, albeit in a very pragmatic manner.
