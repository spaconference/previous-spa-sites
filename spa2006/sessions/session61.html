SPA Conference session: Thinking About The Box

One-line description:Improve your software by designing a shrink-wrapped CD box to put it into
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Workshop [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Business software development is mostly done by project teams that have a captive user base. Perhaps that's why they usually skip the process of defining a target market for their software, unlike product development teams. But perhaps project teams should think about their users, and their users' goals and motivation, in a similar way as product teams do? This workshop shows how designing a shrink-wrapped box for your software can be a fun and productive way of bringing effective user driven design techniques into your project.
 
Audience background:This workshop is aimed at developers, analysts and managers in software project teams who wish to satisfy more of their users more of the time.
 
Benefits of participating:Attendees will come away with an understanding of how they they can improve the software they deliver by gaining a better understanding of their users.
 
Materials provided:Participants will be provided with a set of handouts that provide detailed instructions on each of the workshop activities, and that also reflect the presentation materials to be used.
 
Process:Participants will work alone (or in project groups if there are multiple participants from the same project) to create CD box designs for a current or past project.

Timetable:
Activity / Allotted Time
Introduction / 5 mins
Identifying user groups and goals / 15 mins
Writing the elevator pitch / 15 mins
Designing the box / 15 mins
Exhibition / 15 mins
Wrap up / 10 mins

Art materials in the form of flip charts, marker pens, and scrap paper should be available. The workshop requires a room with plenty of available wall space, where seating or tables can be easily rearranged.
 
Outputs:Flip chart outputs will be displayed in an exhibition to the whole group at the end of the session. Digital photos will be taken and made more widely available (with permission).
 
History:Original session but not an original idea. See also Luke Hohmann's "Innovation Games", e.g. http://www.enthiosys.com/wdocs/12Ways2-ProductBox.pdf
