SPA Conference session: Aggregating the Commentary

One-line description:Why Syndication technology matters to product managers/marketers/developers
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Hot topic [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:A short presentation (and demonstration using tools created by the leader) of how syndication technology is relevant to various parts of an organization. By syndication technology, I mean:

RSS/Atom (syndicated content)
News Aggregators (for consuming syndicated content)
Syndication search (for consuming specific content)
Blogs (for pushing out your own message)

All of this is relevant for people in the following parts of an organization (not limited to a software development organization):

Product Managers
Project Leaders
PR staff
Product Marketers
General Marketing staff

For all of these people, syndication technology is introducing a wild card into how they deal with their prospects and customers - PR staff in particular are being impacted. In general, I tell people that this new space brings this to the forefront:

"Either go out and define your message, or other people will define it for you"
(See Jeff Jarvis and Dell Hell for an example of this).

I expect to give a short presentation, followed by a discussion with the audience.
 
Audience background:Any. People in Leadership (either technical, management, or marketing) should be especially interested
 
Benefits of participating:If your organization (or you) are still not up to speed on what RSS/Atom is all about, then this session will give you that information, and provide you with a forum to ask questions. You'll come away with a better handle on the space, and with pointers to tools for dealing with it (free, open source tools)
 
Materials provided:The presentation, and CD's with the tools used for examples

-- BottomFeeder (An RSS/Atom news aggregator)
-- Silt (A cross platform blog engine)
 
Process:As a hot topic session, I intend to talk for 10-15 minutes, with a few live (assuming a net connection; cached if not) examples of what I'm going on about. That will be followed by questions/discussion from the audience until time is up
 
Outputs:I'll provide a CD with the open source software discussed, and will post about the session on my blog.
 
History:I gave a longer talk on this topic at Smalltalk Solutions 2005 and at ESUG 2005 this past summer. I would like to shorten the talk time and lengthen the discussion time relative to those talks
