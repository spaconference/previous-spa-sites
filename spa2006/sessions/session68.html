SPA Conference session: Getting To Know Your Customer

One-line description:Techniques to help understand your Customers' needs.
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Workshop [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:A software group is best measured by its customers' success. Understanding what they really need is critical, but customers are human too which means that they're fallible. Customers can't always tell you what they want because sometimes they don't know themselves, so asking them to rank requirements or write stories might not be the best place to start.

This workshop presents techniques for working with customers and other stakeholders to help them understand the context and goals of a project or product. We will describe a range of techniques -- such as "Speedboat", "Product Box", "Butterfly Stamping", and "Give 'em a jacuzzi" -- and run two of them as exercises.
 
Audience background:Anyone involved in a software project
 
Benefits of participating:Learn some fun techniques for working with small groups of customers to create innovative products and services.
 
Materials provided:Materials to play the games
 
Process:1 A brief introduction to the various "Getting to Know Your Customer Techniques" we have been using along with the theory behind them.

2. Participants get to experience two of the techniques for themselves (Probably "Product Box" and "Butterfly Stamping").

3. Wrap up with advice on how to run these sessions with real customers.
 
Outputs:poster at conference based on the results of the exercises.
 
History:New session, but msot of the material is based on material from Luke Hohmann which Andy has presented with him.
