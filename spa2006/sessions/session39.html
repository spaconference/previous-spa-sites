SPA Conference session: These are just some of my favorite tools

One-line description:You try out many tools and techniques, which ones are most valuable to you?
 
 Session materials
 
Session format: Working group [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:An ongoing activity for many SPA participants is finding and trying out new tools and techniques for themselves. This concerns technical tools, as well as thinking, process and communication tools. For us, this is one of the main reasons to come to SPA, as there are always sessions on topics we haven't explored yet.

Instead of spending a Sunday on exploring a specific technique, we would like to invite ourselves and others to bring our favourite tools and techniques, to explain what they are (if necessary) to others, and to explore why they work so well for us (and in which context).

The objective of this session is to understand better why and in which context certain techniques work (or don't work) for us, and to learn new techniques from others.

Examples of tools and techniques we have been using ourselves are: value stream mapping, iteration planning, temperature readings, retrospectives, scenario planning, test driven development, systems thinking, blogging, going to the gym, refactoring...
 
Audience background:Anybody who likes to reflect on her or his way of working, enjoys sharing their experience and learning from others'. Basically, anybody who enjoys coming to SPA.
 
Benefits of participating:Understand better why certain techniques work (or don't work) for us, and learn new techniques from others.
 
Materials provided:
 
Process:We will use a process based on open space principles, where participants present their tools and group around the tools they would like to explore.

Timetable:
10 min introduction
15 min participants present their favourite tools very briefly
60 min round 1
30 min present results; switch groups
60 min round 2
30 min present results; switch groups
60 min round 3
30 min present results; switch groups
15 min evaluation + close session
 
Outputs:Posters. Organizers will bring laptops with wiki pages, for those who prefer wikis.
 
History:This session has not been run before. We have experience running open space events (Dutch XP users group and the Agile Open conference) and getting people to talk about what inspires them. We want to draw conversations out of the bar into the conference. Therefore we prefer to spend plenty of time on sunday in a relaxed setting (we understand there is a conference room with couches this time? :-) ).
