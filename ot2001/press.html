PRESS INFORMATION

Conference that tracks the changing face of software
 
 

The OT conference has now been running for nine years – providing a highly interactive environment in which software professionals explore the cutting edge of software practice. It is now firmly established as an event at which new ideas and fresh thinking on software practice emerge.

The conference explores a broad range of leading-edge subjects and issues encompassing technology, pioneering software development and deployment practices, and innovative techniques for managing software projects and the people that make up the project team. In previous years, the conference has made significant contributions to the advancement of frameworks, software architecture, patterns, and internet technology.

The conference has also contributed to educational and learning methods, establishing techniques many of which have since been taken up by other event organisers. Based on a tradition of active participation, the OT event uses a variety of innovative processes for bringing people together to work and learn. The conference promotes an interactive and reflective style that clearly distinguishes it from marketing-led, vendor-driven conferences. Participants are encouraged to share their knowledge and experience, and to learn from others, in a lively and supportive environment

Drama workshops, Simulations, Think-tanks, Goldfish bowls, Working groups, and impromptu Birds of a Feather sessions have all been pioneered and developed at this conference during its 9-year history.
 
 

OT2001 takes place in Oxford, England from 26 to 28 March 2001. XP, EJB, and XML all feature strongly in this year’s conference programme, together with a host of other topics of direct concern to today’s software professionals. In all, more than 40 sessions are scheduled in the formal conference programme, and these will be supplemented by a range of bird of a feather sessions and other meetings arranged during the conference.
