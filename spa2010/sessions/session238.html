SPA Conference session: Building the Testing Pipeline

One-line description:Understand how to layer your tests to maximise feedback
 
Session format: Workshop (75 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Automated testing is one of the corner stone practices for teams practising XP yet there are multiple levels of automated testing to understand. Combined with effective Continuous Integration, automated testing offers rich levels of feedback, assuming you structure them in the right way. During this workshop, participants will better understand the tradeoffs you need to consider when looking at different levels of automated tests. Having learned these tradeoffs, participants will attempt to structure them in a way to maximise the speed and quality of feedback. We will then compare these with different examples from the real world to identify opportunities for improvement.

Participants will plan out their logical testing pipeline using lightweight modelling techniques (i.e. index cards and sticky notes!) to keep everything language and tool agnostic. We will then run a second iteration over the plan, layering a variety of tools and libraries on top to understand what it would look like in the real world.
 
Audience background:Some familiarity with testing concepts (at least some of the testing language)
 
Benefits of participating:Become more familiar with different types of tests
Leverage visualisation techniques to help communicate testing and building pipelines with your team
 
Materials provided:- All materials needed for the workshop
- Slides
 
Process:I'm going to break the audience into small groups. Each group will work through a series of activities including:
1) Identifying types of tests, matching them up with pros and cons. The point is to let people construct their own models given a restricted vocabulary preprinted. They would then need to match up the definition against the terms and then we would compare them to one built previously. It's a guided self discovery.
2) Building a sequential timeline of other activities (e.g. generate compiled distributable, deploy distributable, publish reports, etc), sequencing the types of tests alongside the other set of activities
3) Layering tools on top of logical sequences to relate it to real life testing pipelines

I will probably introduce a scenario before breaking up the groups so that everyone is on the same page. I'll also produce a handout to accompany this to help people keep focused on that scenario.
 
Detailed timetable:0:00 - 0:15 Introduction to some of the testing terms
0:15 - 1:00 Group work (run activities 5 minute intro + 10 minute execution)
1:00 - 1:15 Discussion, Q&A and Wrap Up
