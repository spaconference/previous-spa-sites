SPA Conference session: Aristotle, pragmatism, and software craft

One-line description:Two approaches to the art of software development, grounded in philosophy
 
Session format: Tutorial (75 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:What can programmers learn from the thought of Aristotle, Mill, and other philosophers? More than you might think. Find out what some of the greatest minds in history think about things like craft, art, virtue, and happiness, and how they would run a software project.

In this workshop, we'll link philosophical ethics to the processes and practices of software development, by discussing two important, and divergent, perspectives: Aristotle and pragmatism. Along the way, we'll talk specifically about ethics, political theory, and software process.

Ultimately, we'll ask, and discuss, the following question: is successful software development primarily a matter of creating the right outcomes or cultivating the right practices?
 
Audience background:-- Experience thinking abstractly about software development
-- Experience practicing software development
-- Philosophical training not necessary!
 
Benefits of participating:Participants will discuss, and better understand, the theoretical underpinnings of the practice and craft of software development. They'll hopefully be able to better answer (or ask) questions like: "Why do we do things this way?" and "What makes successful software development?"
 
Materials provided:Slides, bibliography
 
Process:The workshop will be a combination of tutorial and discussion. I'll introduce the issues, present questions, and facilitate discussion.
