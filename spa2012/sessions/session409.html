SPA Conference session: Pattern writing workshop

One-line description:Interactive pattern writing workshop in which people get to write patterns
 
Session format: Workshop (150 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Have an insight? See something important? Want to share it? You are the only one who can share what you know. Patterns can help.

To realise the true power of patterns you have to write them. And writing patterns is the best way to deepen your insight, and address your reader.

In this workshop you will write patterns in miniature. We supply the problem, you supply the solution - and the words. Along the way you will understand how and why patterns work, see their variety, and learn practices that improve your writing skills. Whether you want to write your own patterns, better understand other patterns or improve your writing and critical thinking skills this workshop is for you.

Notes for reviewers:
1. "We supply the problem" - we have a video (about milk) which contains all sorts of solutions and problems to work with.
2. This is more than GoF patterns, we want people to write new patterns. Obviously milk production is about more than the 23, if we open people's minds to that then good.
 
Audience background:Nothing specific, the average SPA attendee will know a bit about these things.
 
Benefits of participating:Experience pattern writing, learn to identify problems, forces, solutions and consequences - the essential elements of a pattern.
Learn to appreciate the content of published patterns and understand how they are structured.
Prepare participants to take part in *PLoP conferences.
 
Materials provided:Tetrapac milk carton video.
 
Process:After a brief introduction to the essential elements of a pattern participants will watch the video. There is then a group discussion about the problems and solutions shown in the video.
Using this raw material the group breaks into smaller working groups who then apply the pattern format to the problems and solutions identified. Facilitators circulate and advise on pattern form and writing.
In the longest version of this workshop participants also take part in a mini-writers workshop; in 150 minutes we might be able to do one of these at most
 
Detailed timetable:10m Introduction
10m Video
20m miscussion
60m pattern writing & shepherding
30m writers workshop
20m wrap up
 
Outputs:Patterns to take home
