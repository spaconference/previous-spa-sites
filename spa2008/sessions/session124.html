SPA Conference session: The Need for Speed

One-line description:A chance to learn about implementing high-performance computing solutions using next-generation hardware and what this means to you.
 
Session format: Tutorial (75 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:We have been involved in several projects investigating and implementing high-performance computing solutions using cutting-edge next-generation hardware (e.g. FPGAs, Array Processors and multi-processor-multi-core CPU systems). Our experience of fulfilling 'the need for speed' shows that the technology choice should not just be based on performance gain; many factors have to be considered from the necessary skills of the developers to the development process; from the development tools available to non-functional requirements such as data-centre space and cooling.

This session is a combination of Tutorial – describing the technologies available and their pros and cons – and Case Study: what we found when we tried to build solutions with them.
 
Audience background:Managers and technologists who would like to gain an appreciation of the emerging high-performance technologies and how it might impact them. Also anyone that just likes cool geeky stuff (Geek Porn).
 
Benefits of participating:Learn about some interesting emerging technologies likely to be in a server near you soon
Learn about the realistic performance benefits these can bring
Learn about their impact on software development
 
Materials provided:Presentation slides
 
Process:Simple slideshow with questions. Planning to bring some hardware. Limited opportunities for hands-on experience in the session but can demo and then do a BoF or bar session for those who really want to play with some code.
