SPA Conference session: Simple, Simplest, or Simplistic?

One-line description:A tutorial about simplicity in software development
 
Session format: Tutorial (150 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Simplicity is a concept that lies at the very foundation of software engineering: simple software is more maintainable, has fewer bugs, has a longer lifetime, etc.

In the last few years, the advent of Extreme Programming and agile methods has made simplicity (e.g., "do the simplest thing that could possibly work") even more fashionable. However, simplicity is an elusive concept, and, very often, people confuse it with the first idea they can think of, producing simplistic solutions that eventually lead to unnecessarily complex software.

The goal of the session is to explore this concept, giving also real-life (good and bad) examples, and stimulate a discussion with the audience about the reasons why we continue to produce unnecessarily complex software, and about what we can do to avoid the traps of simplistic solutions.

 
Audience background:Some exposure to the technical side of software development
 
Benefits of participating:Understanding better what "simple" means in the context of software development, what the dangers of simplistic solutions are, and what we can do to recognise and avoid them.
 
Materials provided:The pdf of the slides
 
Process:It is a tutorial during which the audience can ask questions or provide insights.
 
Detailed timetable:This can be a 150 or 75 minute session--originally it was presented as a 90 minute one, but I can add or remove material from it.

There is no specific timetable, except that, if it is accepted as a 150 minute one, there is the need of a 10-15 minute break in the middle.
