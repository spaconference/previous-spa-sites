ZIP! Becoming Seamless

Tutorial 75 minutes

An efficient integrated process from Stakeholder trigger to Component Architecture

Graham McLeod

Starting above the system level, modeling the interaction of the organization with the environment, reengineering the business processes, validating this with the domain object model, refining it with prototypes and mapping it seamlessly to a multi-tier component based and potentially distributed (Internet?) architecture

 Graham is managing partner of a consulting, training and research organisation (Inspired) and a senior lecturer at the University of Cape Town. He is actively involved in methods and architecture research and practice and chairs an IFIP task group on the evaluation of UML and associated processes.

Topics

A practical and seamless method which integrates:
  • Stakeholder modeling - the view from outside
  • Business Process Engineering - Delivering best service and quality
  • Prototyping - Refining the requirements
  • Domain model mapping - Verifying active and static models
  • Architecture based design mapping - Translating to technology without distortion
  • Design is suitable for multi-tier client server, distributed objects, CORBA/EJB/Com+, Internet deployment

Benefits

  • Get out of the Use Case quagmire.
  • Reduce the number of models produced.
  • Avoid distortion of requirements in translation to technical design.
