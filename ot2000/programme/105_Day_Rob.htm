Romancing The Deadline

Diversion/Play 40 minutes

Finding Love and Satisfaction in Software Development

Rob Day

Nicholas Chagrin

Colston Sanger

Alan Cameron Wills

"The Deadline", Tom de Marco's novella about the joy andthe heartaches of Webster Tompkins, must rank as THE most readable text on software project management yet written. The book reflects Tom's standing as one of the most influential thinkers and authors in the software industry. The play follows Webster on his journey of self-discovery through the labyrinth that is software development in Morovia. matter.

Rob Day (RobDay@compuserve.com)

RDA Ltd.
CONTACT PERSON, CASTING DIRECTOR & CO-SCRIPT WRITER

Rob is an experienced trainer/mentor in OO methods and project management but is prepared to sacrifice his reputation in order to provide OT delegates with the opportunity to see their work in a humourous light. In addition to being crowned on stage with a pointy-hair wig, Rob co-scripted last year's play with Alan Wills.

Nicholas Chagrin (chagrin@compuserve.com)


PRODUCER & ARTISTIC DIRECTOR

Nicholas is an accomplished actor with both large and small screen credits to his name. Nicholas has little interest in the technology of OO but is fascinated by the people aspects of software development. His was the guiding hand that lead to last year's play receiving such rapturous reviews.

Alan Cameron Wills (alan@trireme.com)

TriReme International Ltd.
CO-SCRIPT WRITER

As co-author of the Catalysis blockbuster with Desmond D'Souza, Alan is already a renowned writer. He is less in the public eye as playwright but his critical observations of the IT industry served as the foundation for last year's play. Alan's portrayal of the "French Operations" role was an acting tour de force.

Colston Sanger (colston_sanger@antelopes.com)

Antelope Projects Ltd.
LEAD SCRIPT WRITER

Colston Sanger, aka "Doc Strange", is co-author of "The Programmer's Stone" (an evil rant circa 1997). He scrapes a living as a change management consultant. A relative unknown to OT devotees he has been persuaded to lead the writing of this year's romantic extravagnza.

Topics

    The play will address the following

  • Act 1 The deadline versus the estimate
  • Act 2 Looking for the juicy chunks
  • Act 3 The breakthrough

Benefits

Delegates will leave the play wiser and happier - albeit a little strained from excessive laughter.

Session: Diversion/Play 40 minutes Level: intermediate
Audience: Anybody who has lost their way in software development and,equally, those who derive great fun from it. Max 60

Material

Delegates can purchase signed copies of the book.

Delivery

Rave reviews in "The Stage" and "The Oxford and Warwick Flyfishers' Journal"

Format

A play in three acts - a beginning, a middle and an end.
