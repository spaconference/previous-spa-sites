Using a Component-based Software Factory

Case study 75 minutes

The practical results of an automatedcomponent-based software development project

Richard T. Dué

This Case study gives the practical, project management results of an actual project that used an automated software factory to deliver a new system. The software factory is especially constructed to reliably produce web-enabled, platform independent, e-commerce systems in a 20 week or less timeframe. This case study discusses the use of the software factory from the perspective of a user of the developed system. Special attention is given to the dangers and weaknesses of component based development. The session ends with a discussion of the consequences of the software factory approach to the traditional systems developer and user.

Richard T. Dué (rtdue@ccinet.qb.ca)

Thomsen Dué and Associates Limited
