UML/Catalysis: An Effective Process Improvement

Case study 75 minutes

Specifying Requirements Quickly and Precisely

Greg Urquhart

Nick Outram

Specifying requirements quickly yet precisely is always difficult. Specifying requirements of constantly emerging telecommunications technologies and adapting to changes and fads is a near impossible task. Kingston-SCL have implemented process improvements, based on Catalysis with UML, allowing us to remain at the front of the market.

Applying Catalysis techniques to construct UML models raised many pertinent questions about the requirements. This was done at an early stage in the development when the cost and impact of resolving these issues is significantly less than most organisations incur.

By creating a UML model of our requirements that was much simpler than the implementation, we were able to discuss important requirements much more readily than they could have been in terms of the implementation model.

We were adding new functionality to and amending existing functionality of Jupiter. We did this by mapping our abstract model onto the implementation structure using the 'retrieval' technique. This allowed us to trace requirements through to code updates.

We used a phased development process similar to RUP.
