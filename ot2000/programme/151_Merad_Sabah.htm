Activity Diagrams - So what!

Workshop 170 minutes

How can we make the most of UML activity diagrams in order to capture the business requirements.

Dr Sabah Merad

Business process re-engineering, design and improvement techniques are known to use various notations including flow chart diagrams, which are not standardised. UML activity diagrams can potentially be an excellent tool which helps business analysts to capture business requirements free from use case modelling, but still being within the scope of the UML standard. This workshop explores traditional modelling techniques in the business modelling area, and explains how these techniques can be expressed using standard UML. It also explains how these models can be used as an input to systems development based on a use case modelling approach.

Dr Sabah Merad (sabah@sm-a.co.uk)

SM&A

Her particular interests include business process modelling and analysis, component modelling and development, UML, development methodologies and software architecture.

She has experience of applying traditional and object oriented software development approaches in a variety of project environments. Her skills focus on developing software systems and products from inception through to delivered business benefit.

Her pragmatic approach resulted in establishing an outstanding track record of delivering high quality consulting and training services. She has regularly presented training courses as well as papers and tutorials in various conferences and seminars.

Topics

  • Business modelling - overview and background
  • Activity diagrams - syntax and semantics
  • Use of activity diagrams in business modelling
  • Use of activity diagrams in a component-based development environment

Benefits

  • Enables attendees to explore existing techniques in business modelling
  • Attendees will explore the potential of UML activity diagrams
  • Attendees will learn how to use activity diagrams and will be able to play an active and effective role on projects using business modelling
  • Provides attendees with an understanding of how business modelling techniques are used to support a component-based development process.
  • Provides attendees with an unbiased, balance view of the business modelling notations enabling delegates to make effective use of the techniques in a project environment.

    Format

    Interactive presentation, where delegates will be asked what techniques they are aware of in the business modelling area...

    This is an interactive session. Attendees will be asked to work in groups, in every step of the session. At each key step, a list of findings from each group will be presented to the rest of the class. The presenter will then relate the findings to the proposed solution. Attendees will be asked to make comments. The proposed solution can then be refined accordingly. The presenter is intending to make use of slides, to compare the attendees findings with hers.
