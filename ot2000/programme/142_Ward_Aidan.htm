Transacting Development

Workshop 170 minutes

How much does it cost to exchange the information needed to build a successful system?

Aidan Ward

Susannah Finzi

Everyone has experienced poor relationships and poor communication across the Business/IT interface. This workshop explores how to capture and manage the cost of It is possible to predict at the start of a project the types of change that may happen in the life of a project and the types of re-negotiation that may ensue. The predicted cost of these transactions often invalidates the business case.

Participants will build a cost estimation model to adapt to their own organisation.

Aidan Ward (aidan_ward@antelopes.com)

Antelope Projects Ltd

Aidan has been a risk management consultant with Antelope since 1995. Prior to that he was a software engineering management consultant with Coherent Technology, after an early career as a research consultant in seismic geophysics.

Aidan developed Antelope's Scimitar Risk Management System to allow clients to achieve business objectives in the presence of risk. Unlike most risk management approaches, Scimitar deals with the responsiveness of the organisation and its management system rather than trying to quantify the detailed risks.

Aidan has led risk programmes to board level with organisations including Tesco, BT MNS, Lloyds of London, Norwich Union Healthcare and Willis Corroon.

Topics

  • Gaining and managing perspective
  • Stakeholder responses to risk
  • Data on the cost of negotiating change
  • Mechanisms that reduce the cost of transactions
  • Building and adapting cost models

Benefits

  • You will learn how to focus on the partnership and common goals between customer and supplier.

  • You can then separate the development cost from the cost of reaching the partnership goals.

  • You will practice predicting the cost of successful delivery and managing information transaction costs when they are excessive.
