MiniMethod

Workshop 170 minutes

Defining a minimalist but comprehensive OO method using progressive deliverable states

Graham McLeod

Workshop will try to distill out the essence of what needs to be defined to fully describe a system requirement/design sufficiently for accurate construction of a successful system. The deliverables required will be identified. The process of achieving their definition will then be mapped, detailing the states through which the deliverables pass at each point. These deliverable/state combinations, taken as pre- requisites, yield an efficient minimalist process

Graham McLeod (mcleod@iafrica.com)

Inspired / University of Cape Town

Graham is managing partner of a consulting, training and research organisation (Inspired) and a senior lecturer at the University of Cape Town. He is actively involved in methods and architecture research and practice and chairs an IFIP task group on the evaluation of UML and associated processes.

Topics

  • What constitutes a fully defined system?
  • What deliverables most efficiently encode this information?
  • What states do the deliverables move through in the lifecycle?
  • What are the deliverable/state dependencies?
  • Defining the minimalist process ito these deliverables

Benefits

  • Think about how methods can be optimally constructed to meet a given need.
  • Reduce the number of models produced and hence the project effort
  • Provide a small but effective method definition practitioners can
