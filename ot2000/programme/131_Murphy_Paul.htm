Experiences of Combining Objects, Components and Rules

Case study 75 minutes

Object, Component and Rule: perfect mix or recipe for disaster?

Paul Murphy

The experiences of developing a large, commercial-strength system combining three paradigms - object, component and rule based development. Did this enable the selection of the best points of each or just produce a confused development?

A description of the benefits enjoyed and the problems encountered from this mixture of paradigms.

An exploration of solutions to some of the problems encountered. Inviting solutions from the participants and comparing them with the solutions attempted in the development.

Paul Murphy (Paul.Murphy@CMGPLC.COM)

CMG

I am an I.T. Consultant specializing in the development of object-oriented and component-based systems. My background is in underwriting and systems development for a major composite insurer. I have been involved in object-oriented systems development for five years and component-based systems for two years. My main projects over the last two years have been the development of a systems authoring tool and methodology and the implementation of a system using that tool.

Topics

  • An overview of the project including scope, organisation,architecture, methods and tools.
  • Rule-based development in a commercial environment.
  • The benefits and problems encountered by mixing paradigms.
  • Solutions to the problems encountered in the project.

Benefits

The audience can share the experiences of a commercial development spread across paradigms, seeing both the benefits and conflicts this gave. The session will explore real-life problems and draw solutions from the knowledge and experience of all the participants.

Session: Case study 75 minutes Level: intermediate
Audience:

System architects, designers and developers

Max 30

Material

Presentation and comment forms.

Delivery

Collection and publication of comments and issues raised.

Format

Presentation inviting comment and solutions from the floor.
