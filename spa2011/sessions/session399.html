SPA Conference session: A gentle introduction to the game of Go

One-line description:Get you playing Go in 15 minutes and then for the rest of your life
 
Session format: Workshop (150 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Go is a strategic board game of Chinese origin. Its rules are utterly simple, there are just four of them. Nevertheless the resulting games can be very complex and highly interesting. While in the past Go was considered to be one of the four arts of cultivated gentlemen in the past, these days a lot of Mathematicians and computer scientists seem to gravitate to the game.

The session offers a Go experience for a wide range of participants. Beginners start by playing the corner exercise, i.e. by competing for a single corner of the board instead of playing a full game. For those beyond the corner exercise, different board sizes and a handicap allow for meaningful matches across a wide range of playing skills.

The presenter would *not* expect a paying audience to attend the conference because of this session. Nevertheless, the presenter has experienced people taking a break from more serious topics in the course of previous SPA conferences and could see this session fitting into a late afternoon slot or possibly an evening *diversion*. The duration of the session should not be less than 90 mins though.
 
Audience background:Anybody is welcome to participate, there are no particular prerequisites.
 
Benefits of participating:First of all, learning a fascinating game. Understanding the trade offs to consider when making a move helps improving decision making. Last but not least, observing behavioural patterns in the course of a game provides valuable feedback that can be applied in other contexts as well. The latter two benefits admittedly take some time to materialise but have been experienced the presenter.
 
Materials provided:A few game sets plus software versions of the game. If the session is accepted, the presenter would reach out to local Go groups to get further game sets.
 
Process:After the rules of the game have been explained, the first couple of games is played collaboratively before participants form groups of two and continue playing. Beginners start with the corner exercise.
 
Detailed timetable:00:00 - 00:15 : Explaining the rules of the game
00:15 - 02:30 : Playing games
 
Outputs:None
 
History:This session has been held at an internal ThoughtWorks event in 2010. The presenter actively plays Go, his current rank is 10 kyu.
