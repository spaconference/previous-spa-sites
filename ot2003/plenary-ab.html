A Programmer's Best Friend - eXtreme Programming with a Robot Dog

In producing their affordable consumer robot, the AIBO, the Sony Corporation have often emphasized its basic role as an autonomous pet and describe its function mainly as an Entertainment Robot. Indeed, a paper from the Sony Computer Science Labs in Paris entitled, The role of uselessness in the design of artificial pets indicates further the direction that Sony research is heading. However, while creating a framework for interfacing the AIBO to the Dolphin Smalltalk programming system we have also stumbled across potential future applications for "not-so-useless" autonomous robots.

In this talk we will introduce an interesting application for a "useful" programmer's pet. Extreme Programming (XP) encourages, among other things, the tenets of merciless Refactoring and Pair Programming. Our Dolphin Smalltalk environment has built in refactoring tools together with a code mentoring facility (based around SmallLint) that aims to aid programmers in dynamically assessing the quality of their code. By attaching the AIBO as a "real world" agent of the code mentoring system we have created an automated Pair Programming partner for the lone programmer. While we walk through the code of our robot framework you will see the AIBO offering critical advice on potential bugs or poorly written classes.

While this "programmer's best friend" is not an altogether serious proposition with the limitations of the current technology, one can see how such robotic mentoring might become truly useful in the not too distant future. If nothing else, we've found it can offer a certain amount of light relief for the caffeine crazed programmer hell bent on bashing out just one more class before midnight.

 

Andy Bower

Andy Bower is a Director and founder of Object Arts Ltd, the UK based company responsible for Dolphin Smalltalk. Andy has been a practitioner of Object Oriented Programming since he discovered the "Blue Book" back in 1984 although, by his own admission, he didn't actually get to grips with the Smalltalk language until some ten years later. In 1994, Andy "saw the light" and became a key member of the team that designed and built Dolphin Smalltalk. He is now a "raving" Smalltalk evangelist and, therefore, not someone who should be engaged in conversation at parties!

Blair McGlashan

Blair McGlashan is Chief Technologist at Object Arts Ltd, where he wrote the Dolphin Smalltalk VM; and the SqueakPorts to Windows CE for use on handheld systems: Cassiopeia, HP320LX.
