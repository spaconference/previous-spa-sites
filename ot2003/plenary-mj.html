Plenary Talk: Sunday 30 March, 21:00

Michael Jackson

Concerns: Separations and Compositions

Separation of concerns is a well-recognised principle.

But it is not always easy to say exactly what is, and what is not, a concern. In this talk a notion of concern is presented based on the decomposition of realistic problems into simple subproblems. The composition of these subproblems to obtain a full solution to the original problem is seen to raise further concerns of a different nature.

 

Michael Jackson

"Michael Jackson has had a long and extraordinarily productive career as a software methodologist. In the 1970's, with Jean-Dominique Warnier and Ken Orr, Jackson pioneered the technique of data-driven program design. In that area, his first book, Principles of Program Design (Academic Press, 1975) is a classic. Jackson’s particular flavor of data-driven program design eventually acquired the acronym JSP, for Jackson Structured Programming.

"System Development (Prentice-Hall, 1983) expanded his scope from programs to systems. It was, in many ways, the first book on object-oriented program design -- Jackson System Development (JSD) was object-oriented before object-oriented was cool.

"In Software Requirements and Specifications (Addison-Wesley, 1995) Jackson expanded his scope again, from information systems to all kinds of computerized systems.

"Problem Frames (Addison-Wesley, 2001) expands on the subject of problem frames introduced in SRS. Over the years, Jackson has rightly become suspicious of 'methodologies' and the often-extravagant claims of their proponents. One of the themes of SRS and Problem Frames is that, when it comes to specifying computer systems, one size does not fit all. There is no single Best Methodology. Rather, software development methods are like tools in a toolbox. Part of the task of the software developer – as with any other kind of craftsman – is to pick the right tool for the job."

-- from a review by Stephen Ferg
