SPA Conference session: Somewhere Over the Rainbow: Discover what being agile feels like

One-line description:The ultimate test of personal agility
 
Session format: Workshop [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Sadly, all too often, Agile adoption is all talk. The true meaning of Agile gets lost amidst so much rhetoric and handwaving from Agile careerists and practitioners alike.

To truly understand what being Agile means and all the benefits that it brings, we need to experience it for ourselves. To live it, breathe it and feel it.

As luck would have it, one of the most Agile groups in existence is the Ukulele community. Come along and experience the transformative power of Agile through play, music and sing-a-long.*

Session Objectives:
- Experience firsthand what living the Agile values and applying the Agile principles feels like
- Reflect on how agile you think you are and what you do in practice, especially when put under pressure to perform
- Develop networking skills
- Make friends
- Have fun!

NOTE TO ORGANISERS:
This session could become a unique selling feature of SPA known as \\\"SPA Jam\\\", an awesomely fun and playful social activity where all conference attendees are encouraged to take part and network in a way never done before at SPA.

I know of a uke troupe who lend out ukes and will join the jam for an affordable fee to make taking part even easier!
 
Audience background:* No prior skill or experience of singing nor ukulele required. Simply bring along your courage, your uke and tuner or your instrument of choice to live and breathe Agile and play for your life!
 
Benefits of participating:- Learn how to play a uke or an instrument of your choice
- Gain confidence by performing in public
- Embrace the yearning to play
- Exercise an existing musical talent
- Witness, experience and exceed personal expectations of what can be achieved by a group of enthusiasts - aka magic!

 
Materials provided:Uke play music
 
Process:A mixture of individual exercises and group play
 
Detailed timetable:This session would work well between 75 - 90 mins (the longer the timebox, the more time to jam):

00:00 - 00:05 - Introduction
00:05 - 00:10 - Personal assessment of personal agility
00:10 - 00.40 - Music jam 1
00.40 - 00.45 - Re-assessment of personal agility
00:45 - 00.75 - Music jam 2
00:75 - 00.90 - Reflection: Insights gained and shared
 
Outputs:A poster of insights gained and shared when our personal agility is put to the test
 
History:A brand new session led by uke novices and, optionally, a supporting uke troupe: The Fleas
