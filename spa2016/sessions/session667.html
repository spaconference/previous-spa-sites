SPA Conference session: The answer to how is why - developing criteria for choosing activities in retrospectives

One-line description:A hands-on session to analyse different retrospective activities and why and when they work.
 
Session format: Workshop 150 minutes (also pos [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:There are countless suggestions for activities to liven up retrospectives that you can find in a number of books and webpages.They are all (probably) useful in a certain context - but when should one use which one?
Obviously people use gut feeling and common sense, but it would be nice, if there were a few guidelines.
This session aims at working towards this. The outcomes will be collected and evaluated towards creating a collection of "retrospective activities" that not only describe the "how"- but also the "why" of these activities.
 
Audience background:- every one attending Spa who actually wants to connect and is curious about people
 
Benefits of participating:- better understanding of what makes retrospective activities work
- reflect on their own process for choosing "retrospective activities
- Having fun
- being part of building a toolbox with not only the tools to use but the Information when to use them
 
Materials provided:
 
Process:A hands-on session to analyse different retrospective activities and why and when they work.
The participants will work together in groups of 2-4 to analyse some retrospective activities and answer questions such as:
- what problem does this "activity" solve?
- are there specific contexts where this "activity" works better than others?
- what are the benefits, limitations and liabilities of using this "activity"?
 
Detailed timetable:- 00:00 - 00:05 Introduction
- 00:05 - 00:15 brainstorming on what is important to participants when deciding on activities
- 00:15 - 00:20 brainstorming on different retrospective activities
- 00:20 - 01:00 group work on different activities
- 01:00 - 01:15 presentation of results
