How to Get Ahead in Architecture

Workshop session at Object Technology 98

Tuesday 7th April, 15:00

David Harvey
 

No Richard E Grant, I'm afraid, and moreover this will help only indirectly in career advancement as a software architect. But this workshop will explore ways in which architecture can be introduced with confidence of success, and will capture this experience as a pattern language... 

 

Many commentators on software architecture write as if introducing new systems and architectures were a simple matter. While some organisations offer green-field opportunities for the introduction of new architectures, it is much more common to have to work around existing systems, projects, organisational structures and cultures. How to Get Ahead in Architecture will specifically explore issues surrounding the introduction of an architecture into an existing environment. 

Objectives

  • Identify key issues surrounding architecture implementation. These will include the influence of adopting an architecture on development process and culture, in addition to issues concerned directly with technology choice and implementation
  • Share experience of addressing these issues
  • Express the issues and their solutions as a pattern language
  • Explore the effectiveness of the workshop's process as a way of arriving at a first-cut of such a pattern language

Who should attend?

Anyone involved in developing large systems, whether architect, designer, developer or manager. Delegates with any level of experience are welcome. Participants should have some familiarity with pattern concepts. 

Output

The session will express the contexts, issues and resolutions in pattern form. The output of the session will consequently be a draft of a pattern language for architecture introduction into existing environments. This will be summarised on a poster and written up for publication in the BCS OOPS newsletter. 

Follow-up

Participants will be encouraged to adopt and refine individual patterns. A short period of e-mail review will be followed by a joint presentation of the pattern language at a BCS OOPS event. 

Structure of the workshop

The workshop will be intensive and highly interactive. Presentation will be limited to introducing and motivating the session's principal question, and stating requirements for expressing resolutions to the issues raised. All other work will be in small groups (three participants) or in group discussion.
