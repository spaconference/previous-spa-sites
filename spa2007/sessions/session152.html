SPA Conference session: And you thought you could estimate ?

One-line description:Measuring our abilities of dealing with estimation, planning, requirements, assumptions, design, test, defects, and interruptions, and what we can do to improve.
 
Session format: Tutorial (150 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:We all know that projects are always late and deliver a lot of defects. We know everything so well and it’s always “they” and “them” who make a mess of projects. In this tutorial we will do an exercise to measure our own abilities of dealing with estimation, planning, requirements, assumptions, design, test, defects, and interruptions, to see whether we really are any better than “they”. After the exercise we will talk about how the Evolutionary Project Management (Evo) approach is designed to quickly and effectively improve our abilities to deal with these issues in order to run our projects more successfully, with less defects and in much shorter time. Finally, we do an exercise organizing our own next week's work the Evo way. Learning to know what you can promise and then living up to your promises takes the quicksand from under your project.
 
Audience background:• Higher management, responsible for the results of their organization.
• R&D managers, responsible for the results of their department.
• Project managers, responsible for the results of their project.
• Software developers, responsible for the results of their work.
• Quality management, responsible for optimizing the quality of the results.
 
Benefits of participating:Experiencing our own weaknesses of dealing with estimation, planning, requirements, assumptions, design, test, defects, and interruptions is a first step of doing something about it.
Understanding why and how the Evolutionary approach supports projects to be routinely successful and on time.
Experiencing how to organize your next weeks work the Evo way.
You may even, after the conference, get some coaching-on-a-distance (if you can keep the discipline) about keeping your next weeks organized.
 
Materials provided:• Handout of the presentation
• 4 booklets related to the subject
 
Process:First we do the exercise, where people will understand their weaknesses in many software development practices. Then I will explain some, mainly psychological reasons for these weaknesses and how we can do something about it. Discussion is encouraged. At the end we'll do a second exrcise, planning our own work of the next week the Evo way. This will show how to put the theory into your own practice.
 
Detailed timetable:13:00 - 13:10 Introduction
13:10 - 13:40 Estimation exercise
13:40 - 14:15 Issues and human behaviour elements we should be aware of
14:15 - 14:45 Break
14:45 - 15:30 How Evo pragmatically solves a lot of the issues
15:30 - 15:45 Planning our own next week's work the Evo way
15:45 - 16:00 Discussion and conclusion
 
Outputs:People go home with their next week's work organized. If they like, and can keep the discipline (this is the really hard part!) they can get a few weeks free coaching on keeping their week's work organized. During this coaching, they learn a lot more, the hard way.
 
History:I've done the estimation exercise since september 2006 at least 10 times now, at conferences (Israel, Japan, Amsterdam, Gilb2007, USA) and with Evo introductions at clients, and it always works. It proves to be a rather humiliating, or let's call it a revealing experience. Still, because people deep in their hart know, they always see the outcome as a good learning experience. The tutorial I've done numerous times, although every time honed based on ongoing experiences. Organizing the work of the next week I'm teaching all the time, since about 2001. Coaching on a distance I've done several times, since about 2005.
