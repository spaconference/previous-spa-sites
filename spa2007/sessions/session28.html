SPA Conference session: Exploring Metamodelling

One-line description:Introduction and exploration of metamodelling focussing on its use in OMG and ISO standards
 
Session format: Tutorial (150 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:Metamodelling is often viewed as obscure and irrelevant for the daily work of software developers. To dispel this misconception this tutorial will demonstrate current tech-niques that make metamodelling affordable to developers and much more applicable and useful for their needs. The techniques explained will include not only basic ideas but also more recently introduced notions of powertype-based metamodelling, the con-struction of methodologies using resources and templates from a method engineering perspective, the integration of process and modelling issues in the same metamodel and the incorporation of capability/maturity attributes at the methodological level. As well as OMG standards such as UML and SPEM, the new ISO/IEC 24744 standard will be used in illustration.
 
Audience background:• The attendees should be familiar with the concept of an object-oriented methodology and have some practice in object-oriented modelling.
• Experience in deploying object-oriented methodologies would be helpful.

 
Benefits of participating:Attendees will understand basic metamodelling and be able to apply new metamodel-ling techniques such as powertype patterns, resources and templates, and proc-ess/product integration. They will receive insights into not only current OMG standards but also new ISO standards, particularly 24744 (Software Engineering Metamodel for Development Methodologies).
 
Materials provided:Presentation slides
 
Process:This tutorial will be lecture-based. Since there are many talking points, audience ques-tions will be expected and invited throughout the presentation. As part of the dialogue, we will jointly construct an FAQ that could be hosted on a website for wider knowledge distribution.
 
Detailed timetable:
 
Outputs:Construction of a FAQ on metamodelling for inclusion on website (can be hosted at http://www.open.org easily)
 
History:This tutorial contains some elements from various tutorials presented at the UML 2002, ER 2003, CAiSE 2004, ECOOP 2004 and APCCM 2005 conferences and to in-dustry groups as well. In this new form, it is to be presented at NODe in Erfurt, Ger-many in late September 2006.
