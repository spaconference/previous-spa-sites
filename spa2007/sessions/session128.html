SPA Conference session: Mirror, mirror on the wall... why me?

One-line description:How to apply Lean software development principles to the individual
 
Session format: Workshop (150 mins) [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:“We must become the change we want to see” - Mahatma Gandhi.

This is your chance to learn more about yourself in a way that truly matters. This session operates on the correlation between personal and team effectiveness by demonstrating how Lean software development principles can translate across to personal development.

You will get to experience a range of complementary self-management and thinking tools that will help you become a more effective self-organising individual and contributor.

The following techniques will be covered:

1. Speed networking – peer-to-peer networking at elevator speed
2. Self-diagnosis using Myers Briggs
3. Productivity survey – how does your working day go?
4. Managing your task list - with the GTD approach
5. Outcome thinking
6. Brainstorming with mind maps and the Six Thinking Hats

There will be a number of practical exercises to allow participants to try out some of the techniques for themselves.
 
Audience background:For software practitioners interested in improving their soft skills and becoming more effective as a Lean individual and team player.
 
Benefits of participating:You will get to experience a range of complementary self-management and thinking tools that will help you become a more effective self-organising individual and contributor.
 
Materials provided:Slides
Survey for completion
Role play scenario
 
Process:This is a highly interactive session with techniques being described and then individual or group exercises being set to allow people to try them out.
