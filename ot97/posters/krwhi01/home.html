Making the Transition to Object-Think

Tuesday, April 15th, 10:45 - 12:00 Session leader: Katharine Whitehead

Objectives of this Tutorial

When we change programming language, we use the new language for a time as if it were the old one. In the same way, when a move to OO is made, there is a period during which existing assumptions must be reassessed for relevance. At the end of this period, our mindset has changed and we use the new paradigm without translating between new and old concepts.

The objective of this tutorial is to make explicit some of the transitions that are often made gradually over a period of time, as the object-oriented paradigm is absorbed. We will discuss conceptual differences between an object-oriented approach and traditional approaches that are often not immediately obvious to people starting to use OO.

The session will focus on language-independent issues, that are of interest particularly at the analysis and design levels.

Issues for Discussion

This session will address the following topics:

  1. How the use of OO requires us to rethink where we draw the line between analysis and design, and what we mean by analysis and by design.
  2. OO uses a real-world analogy to structure systems. Objects, we say, are like objects we find in the real world. This analogy is the source of a number of pitfalls for the newcomer to OO. Why, and what are these pitfalls?
  3. Many of us have worked for years with data modelling techniques, and are told, when we start working with OO, that we overemphasise the importance of data in the definition of objects. Why? What are the differences? Where do we need to adapt our ideas, and where could the OO people learn a thing or two?
  4. One area where existing data modelling knowledge comes into conflict with OO results from the similarity between inheritance and generalization/specialization. What are the differences? How should we think about inheritance? Some of the issues will be explored.

Discussion

There will be time for discussion after each topic has been presented. Attendees are invited to:

  • comment on agreements/disagreements with the point of view presented
  • add points that have not been covered.

At the end of the session, we will ask whether there are other issues that cause major problems when transitioning to OO and whether the difficulties of making the transition are overrated or underrated.

Attendees

Both beginners and more advanced practitioners will be welcome.

Note

This session was previously advertised as a Goldfish Bowl session but will in fact be run as a Tutorial.
