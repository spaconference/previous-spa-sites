Domains, Roles, and Services

1. Service-Based Architecture

Services describe the business processes or tasks that we need to accomplish. The service based architecture describes the system from the point of view of the tasks that the system must perform in order to help the business accomplish its requirements. The application controls which business function occurs when and it also controls the flow of control between the tasks.

2. Roles

The roles based approach to describing systems stresses that there are entities that know how to do the work and that they cooperate to do it; they work together. It is only within the context of their containing role model that the roles' characteristics and behavior make sense. In addition, roles are not considered in isolation; the role model provides the boundary and roles within that boundary interact with each other. Their collaboration is feasible because they know about each other; any individual role does not accomplish all that it needs to - it relies on other roles for some things.

Encapsulation is preserved because the roles do not care how their collaborating roles do the work; only that a role is available that will do what the initiating role wants.

Roles can describe the kinds of things that occur when discussing business concepts.

Finally, roles provide recursive decomposition, because each role can itself be a role model.

3. Domains

Domains are "... a set of object types together consituting a coherent sub-system which can be meaningfully considered as a group."
