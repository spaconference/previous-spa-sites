Introduction

The subject of this session is an approach to architecture that is currently being used within a large (over 30 people) Smalltalk project at J.P. Morgan. A corporate standard founded on service-based architecture already existed, but this does not adequately address the object oriented viewpoint. By integrating Syntropy , and the OORAM approach we have put together a methodology for building a systems architecture.

This new architecture brings together three different approaches to modeling systems: domains, roles, and services; these are detailedbelow. These approaches have in the past been described separately. By considering the concepts within these approaches as well as how they relate, a coherent architecture emerges which takes advantage of the three individual approaches, but also provides additional potential through their synthesis.

Objectives

The objective of the session is to provide the participants with experience in establishing a distributed architecture by making use of various architectural principles and patterns.This will be accomplished via a case study from the financial industry. An additional goal is to illustrate how the combination of some powerful concepts results in an even more effective architecture via their synergy.
