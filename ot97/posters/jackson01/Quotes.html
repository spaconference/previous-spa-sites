Interesting quotes about architecture

"Software architecture deals with the structuring paradigms, styles and patterns that describe, or make up, our software systems" [Dyson].

"....any definition is unnecessarily limiting." [Dyson]

"...an architecture deals with the non-functional, organisational, aspects of the software system..." [Dyson]

"... architecture subdisciplines [are] representation, style, domain specific software architectures, process, and infrastructure..." [Gacek94]

"Achieving a software life-cycle architecture is the single most important milestone in the software process. If you don't have a good architecture, you shouldn't proceed to build a software product." [Gacek94]

"The term software architecture has been defined in many different ways by different people." [Gacek94]

"...a structure composed of components, and rules characterizing the interaction of these components" [Gacek94 referencing [Jones93]]

"A software architecture should identify the major risks, costs and size involved of the product to be developed" [Gacek94].

"...the architecting stage acts as a bridge between requirements analysis and software design..." [Gacek94]

"Software architectures should clearly show the major dependencies between system constraints. This provides a consistency of form, and partitions the complexity. The architecture should be used to define the interfaces between components, and furthermore be considered as a basis for software maintenance in order to prevent architectural erosion and drift." [Gacek94 referencing [Perry92]]

"As systems grow in size and complexity, it becomes increasingly important at the architecting stage to specify more than the topological structure of the product." [Gacek94]

"Software architectures act as an integration mechanism for assembling software reuse components" [Gacek94].

"Architecture is concerned with the selection of architectural elements, their interactions, and the constraints on those elements and their interactions necessary to provide a framework in which to satisfy the requirements and serve as a basis for the design." [Perry92]

"A unifying or coherent form or structure" [F.C. Mish Webster's New Ninth Collegiate Dictionary, Merriam Webster, Springfield, MA, 1983.

"The primary focus in architecture is the identification of important properties and relationships..." [Perry92]

"...although architectural structures may themselves abstract away from details of the actual computations of the elements, those structures provide a natural framework for understanding broader system-level concerns, such as global rates of flow, patterns of communication, execution control structure..." [Shaw95]

"...skeleton around which system properties can be fleshed out..." [Shaw95]

References:

[Dyson] Paul Dyson and Bruce Anderson , "Issues in Software Architecture".

[Gacek94] Cristina Gacek, Ahmed Abd-Allah, Bradford Clark, Barry Boehm, USC Center for Software Engineering - "Focused Workshop on Software Architectures: Issue Paper", April 1994.

[Gacek95] Cristina Gacek, Ahmed Abd-Allah, Bradford Clark, Barry Boehm, USC Center for Software Engineering - "On the Definition of Software System Architecture", April 1995.

[Jones93] A.K. Jones, "The Maturing of Software Architecture", Software Engineering Symposium, Software Engineering Institute, Pittsburgh, PA, August 1993.

[Perry92] D.E. Perry and A.L. Wolf, "Foundations for the Study of Software Architecture", Software Engineering Notes, ACM SIGSOFT, vol. 17, no. 4, October 1992.

[Shaw95] Mary Shaw and David Garlan, "Formulations and Formalisms in Software Architecture", Springer-Verlag Lecture Notes in Computer Science, vol. 1000, 1995.
