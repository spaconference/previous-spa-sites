About this session

Structure

  1. Introduction to us and the workshop
  2. Discussion on what people think architecture is and the goals people have in establishing an architecture.
  3. Review the main points of the individual.
  4. Our approach to how the components fit together to form the synthesized architecture. We also describe the various views onto the architecture, which illustrate the relationship between the ideas.
  5. Introduction of in-depth example introducing problems.
  6. Break-out into groups to establish architecture, following structure given in example. Will come up with concrete views illustrating relationship between the concepts in concrete terms.
  7. Recursively apply decomposition to subset of views, highlighting convergence of the approaches at different levels.
  8. Move to where implementation is very next step for one small part.
  9. Discussion of how helpful this approach was and suggestions for modification. How might different situations result in a focus on one approach (e.g. domains, roles, or services) being beneficial.

Deliverables

  1. Completion of the various views for the example.
  2. Completion of successive decomposition of a subset of views.
  3. Illustration of how transformation will result in implementation.

Target Audience

This session is aimed at people who have had some experience working on an OO project, either as a developer, a designer, or an architect. It will hopefully have several people who have attempted to (either successfully or unsuccessfully) establish an architecture for a new or existing system. There will also hopefully be a range of experience levels. This session should provide, across experience levels, a better understanding of how to go about establishing an architecture and what various concepts that are made use of when people do so.
