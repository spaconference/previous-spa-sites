What is an Architecture?

We would like one that is coherent and provides a base for development.

But what is it? And how do you go about defining one? What should it look like?

Simply stated, the architecture is the structure of the system, consisting of the major components and their interactions. This definition seems to be pretty much accepted. (but what you may ask is a component? ah...) What should these components look like? Are they layers? Frameworks? Something else? And how do you describe their interactions?

How about all the different kinds of Architecture? There's technical architecture, application architecture,information architecture, business architecture, not to mention logical, physical, executable architectures. Each of these has its own viewpoint that it brings to Architecture.

Pick any of these, and it is likely that there will still be some amount of defining to do as to exactly what comprises that kind of architecture.

What are the concepts that we would like our architecture to express? Why do we want an architecture in the first place? We mentioned that it should provide a guide for development above. But in what way? Why else do we want one?

How are we going to make use of An Architecture? Is it something that should be a first class citizen when talking about software engineering?

I suggest that in order to make YOUR architecture useful, you need to have an idea of how it is going to be used, and incorporate that use into your development process. Who exactly needs to use it? What should they get from it?

I also suggest that you need different views to present the various perspectives within an architecture.

Below are some thoughts and questions about these subjects as well as quotes from people who have been thinking about software architecture for awhile. All of these are intended to make you think and formulate some of your own answers. There is a discussion page available - please post your thoughts!

Why / motivation

What are the goals of having an architecture? What do we want to get out of it? What are the benefits?

We expect alot out of having an architecture in place....

  • Facilitate communication between team members and sub-groups
  • Guide for design
  • Baseline for development
  • Elucidate dependencies between efforts
  • Assist with planning and risk management (get a handle on scope of changes)
  • Establish separation of concerns
  • Make system more extensible and maintainable
  • Improve understanding
  • Serve as educational tool for newcomers to team
  • Provide information, enabling technical decisions to be made (e.g. distribution)
  • Serve as basis for reuse
  • Finally... a VISION for the project (something nice and manageable)

Who are the various users of the architecture and how will they use / benefit from it? Consider:

  • designer
  • developer
  • project manager
  • end-user

How should the architecture be used going forward once it is defined and in place?

  • Leave it in a drawer?
  • Really incorporate into development process, resulting in a real "Architecture driven approach"?

Now that we know what we want from it, what is an architecture anyway?

Whose architecture...

Consider the different aspects of architecture...

  • Logical / Physical
    • Logical: conceptual
    • Physical: placement of processes, kinds of interprocess communication
  • Static / Dynamic
  • Architectural Style
    • Object-Oriented is classified as an architectural style (as is Event-Based, Layered, Domain-Specific, Distributed)
  • Technical / Application
  • Executable

What concepts do we want to express in an architecture?

  • Architectural elements and their interactions
  • Structure of application
  • Abstraction of design elements
  • Constraints
    • Properties: constrain the choice of architectural elements
    • Relationships: constrain interactions of elements
  • Rationale behind decisions: is it enough to know what this is / how system requirements (functional, non-functional) are satisfied or does this need to be incorporated into the architectural language?
  • Expose similar mechanisms across business situations
  • Show major dependencies between system components
  • Consistency of form
  • Way of partitioning complexity
  • Appropriate division of responsibilities
  • Overall structure
  • Properties of software systems
  • Conceptual integrity
  • Execution environment
  • Non-functional requirements
  • Principles

Describing these concepts ...

How do we talk about an architecture? In particular, the scope of this workshop concentrates on conceptual architecture.

An architectural description language can help here... You have to come to the workshop to hear about this! But some hints :

  • domains
  • roles
  • services
