Architecture, Infrastructure, Framework - What's in a word?

A think-tank at Object Technology 97

Organiser: John Daniels, Object Designers Limited

architecture n. art or science of building; thing built, structure; style of building; construction

infrastructure n. subordinate parts of an undertaking, esp. permanent installations as basis for military etc. operations

framework n. frame, structure, upon or into which casing contents can be put (lit. or fig.)

Concise Oxford Dictionary

It's very fashionable in object-technology circles to draw comparisons between the activities of writing software and constructing buildings. The word 'architecture' crops up very often in writings and presentations on object-oriented systems but the exact relationship between software design and the activities I ascribe to building architects is not obvious - not to me, at least.

The objective of this think-tank is to agree definitions of the three words shown above, in the context of designing and building object-oriented systems. In the course of doing that I expect considerable insight into the activities of software design, the use of object technology, and the allocation of duties to team members.

Questions I expect to arise include:

  • How do I know whether I have 'an architecture'?
  • How do I communicate an architecture to others?
  • What's the difference between an architecture and an infrastructure?
  • What's the difference between an infrastructure and a framework?
  • What's the difference between an architecture and a pattern language!?

The think-tank will produce three posters, each containing an agreed definition of one of the above words (plus, probably, a commentary on the appropriate interpretation of the definition).

Who should come?

I envisage that the think-tank will comprise people who routinely use these words in their work! These are likely to be system designers, system builders and - errmm - system architects.
