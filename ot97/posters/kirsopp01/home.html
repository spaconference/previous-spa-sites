Object-Oriented Design Heuristics: a BOF Session

Session Leaders

Colin Kirsopp
Steve Webster


Target Audience

This BOF session is aimed at those conference attendees who would consider themselves as being at a beginner or intermediate level at object-oriented design. Some familiarity with OMT/UML would also be preferable

Session Objectives

  • To help less experienced OO designers improve their understanding of object-oriented design.
  • To show how heuristcis can be used to improve designs.
  • To discuss issues concerning the application of heuristics in practice.

Structure of Session

The structure of the session will be based around a design assessment/revision case-study. Attendees will be provided with: a 'base-line' set of heuristics; the case-study specification; and a first draft design solution to the case-study. The group will then assess and revise the first draft design using the given set heuristics. This process of design revision will be used as a catalyst for discussion of the wider issues of OO design heuristics. The direction of the session will be led by the interests of the participants.

Some possible areas of interest/discussion:
  • The validity of the suggested heuristics
  • Suggestions for further heuristics
  • The generality/specificity of heurstics
  • The objectivity/subjectivity of heuristics
  • Trade-offs between heuristcis
  • The process of heuristic application
  • The position of heuristics within the software design process
The session will conclude with a consideration of the state of practice and research in the area of OOD heuristics. This will include: discussion of the existing literature; discussion of current practice in design assessment and the exent of the use of heuristcis; and suggestions for further work in the area.

Session Deliverables

A poster to convey the session results/discussion to other conference delegates.
