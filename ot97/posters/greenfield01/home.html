Developing a Business Processing Framework
WS19

David Greenfield, Cumulus Systems Ltd.

 

Workflow Management Coalition defines a Business Process as

A set of one or more linked procedures or activities which collectively realise a business goal, normally within the context of an organisational structure defining functional roles and relationships.

The Problem: Part 1
Focusing on business processes can lead to bad design

 

"Using business processes as the basis for the design of a system is like forcing the characters in a novel to fit the plot: it may be a good read, but no one will believe in the end result."

The Problem: Part 2
Ignoring business processes can lead to bad analysis

"Building a system without first considering its business processes is like starting to write a novel when you have no idea of what its plot will be: people will get lost trying to understand what is going on."

 

We will consider whether it is possible to address these (and other issues) by building a Business Processing Framework. Explicitly we will consider:

  • Is it possible to build a framework for business processing?
  • How complex would such a framework be?
  • What advantages would it bring to initial application development?
  • How would it affect subsequent maintenance?

 

If you would like to know more, please attend the workshop.
