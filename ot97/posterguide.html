What's in it:

Deal with a single issue
It's OK to make several posters.
One A4 self-contained summary page and 1-5 subsidiary pages each of around A4 length detailing each sub-issue.
The intention is to give people a predictable amount of reading to do when they decide to go down into your poster and each level of detail.
Links to more material and papers
hosted on your own or other sites
Be pictorial, and bullet-like
but bear in mind that access may be slower than from your local server.
Line drawing, sans-serif labels, and block colour are best --- dithering, hatching, dots, and photos take bandwidth.
Some screens are only 640*480.

Suggested parts

Why this is a good solution

How to produce it

We'd like the poster to be in HTML.

If you haven't already been through the business of producing a web page, the easiest way is to produce it in a tool you know, and translate. Most document processing tools now have html translators. For example,

Word Internet Assistant
Free download
Powerpoint Internet Assistant
Free download

Using standard tool+translator means that it is easy to produce a reasonable printed version.

Should you happen to use a sophisticated DTP tool like FrameMaker or QuarkExpress, consider using Word for the present purpose unless you have already used one of their web translators.

Style tips

  • Use tables with 0-thickness borders to make things less linear.
  • Use decorative graphics even if you can't think of any illustrations; but keep them thin or block-like.
  • Ensure your translator includes the width and height attributes in image references.
  • It may be useful to start by modifying an existing poster.
  • If all else fails, at least send us some ASCII !
  • In case of great difficulty, contact Alan Wills

Using it

Before the conference

Check regularly the debate page that you'll find linked from your own. Readers will attach comments (we hope!)

At the conference

If your first page fits on one A4 sheet when printed from Netscape, we will post it at the conference. Otherwise, please bring a printed version yourself.

After the conference

Several session types (working groups, workshops, and goldfish-bowls) include a commitment to deliver some output, so that:

  • others can benefit from the results of the discussions;
  • the discussion can continue afterwards, and perhaps lead to continuing working groups
The poster site will provide a focus for this consequential activity.

You will be asked to provide an electronic summary of the session's deliverables at the end of the conference. (Feel free to delegate to session participants! All benefit.)


How to send it to us

If you have a (reliable) web site of your own, mount the files there and send email:
