Navigating the issues of Distributed Object Technology

Adrian Kunzle
Distributing objects adds a further level of complexity into any application design. What areas really need to be thought about, what can be ignored, and when should these decisions be made. 
One issue is locality --- although you could claim this is

just a performance issue.



The obvious [being provocative!] starting point is the 

structure of the company. Everyone has their machine on

their desk, so objects used by a given person -- and the

object representing that person and the role(s) they play ---

should be located idelly within their machine. 

Departmental objects are in the Department's machine --- 

and so on. 



(Does this make sense? Is it important?)
