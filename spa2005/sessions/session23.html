SPA2005 session: Curly Bracket Tikka Masala

One-line description:How will offshore outsourcing impact IT practitioners in the UK, Europe and US?
 
Session format: Goldfish bowl [read about the different session types]
 
Abstract:One of the current trends in the IT industry is to outsource software development work "offshore" to economies that have lower wages and overhead costs. If successful, such strategies will change the way that large, corporate software is developed with the bulk of the code written "offshore". If this happens, it will alter or eliminate the requirement for skills currently provided by native IT practitioners in the high-wage economies. This session seeks debate, opinions and potentially answers to the following questions:

Is it a good idea?
What needs to be done to make it work successfully?
Are there any showstoppers?
Will it deliver the cost benefits expected or will the cost burden just shift elsewhere (e.g. communication)?
Is outsourcing of development to cheaper places inevitable?
What implications does this have for the role of IT practitioners in the high-wage countries (UK, Europe, USA)?

 
Audience background:Anyone involved in IT and hoping to have a job in IT over the next few years
 
Benefits of participating:- Share insights into how well offshore outsourcing works
- Exchange opinions on pros and cons
- Decide on career direction :-)
 
Materials provided:Initial Speakes, Video camera to record session
 
Process:Typical goldfish bowl debate (5 chairs - 1 always empty - people join from the surrounding "audience" to contribute) Record key points during debate on flip charts.
