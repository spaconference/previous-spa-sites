Published writers at SPA2005

SPA2005 has sessions led by the authors and co-authors of some of the works that may well be on your bookshelves at home and in the office. Come and talk to published thoughtleaders such as:

  • Alistair Cockburn Agile Software Development: Software Through People
  • Suzanne Robertson Mastering the Requirements Process
  • John Daniels UML Components: A Simple Process for Specifying Component-based Software
  • Paul Dyson and Andy Longshaw Architecting Enterprise Solutions: Patterns for High-Capability Internet based systems
  • Michael Feathers Working Effectively with Legacy Code
  • Dan Haywood Better Software Faster
  • Benedict Heal Introduction to Object Oriented methods
  • Richard Pawson and Robert Matthews Naked Objects
  • Alan Cameron Wills Cutting code from UML: Catalysis in Practice
  • Eoin Woods and Nick Rozanski Software Systems Architecture: Viewpoint Oriented Software Development
All of these experts attend SPA2005 because they know its interactive style gives them the best chance to learn as well as teach.
