Whither eProcess?

Wednesday 11:45   Tutorial 75 minutes

On the evolution of business process modelling to web based commercial systems developments, and the subsequent adaptation of development styles to deliver the systems.

Christopher Simons

The arrival of commercial systems trading over the internet has led to an evolution of business process modelling and development styles. Traditionally, business process modelling has driven commercial systems developments by describing what the system is to deliver. Business process models have been typically used within an business enterprise where in-house business experts are available to consult on requirements nuances, where the system is to be deployed in-house, and trained user are specialists on their business domain. Development processes have evolved to analyse, design and incrementally release versions of software to in-house users with appropriate training. More recently, multiple delivery channels bring a range of evolving user interfaces to static legacy systems. As specialist in-house expert users disappear, who are the users? What business models should be produced for web applications? Does the business process drive the construction of the web application or does the application become the business process? Or vice versa? How is meaningful prototyping and user acceptance testing conducted? How can the development process be adapted to minimise the impact of transient Web page based interfaces on components wrapping legacy databases?

Christopher Simons

Currently a Systems Analyst at NatWest involved in the development of component based multi-tier client / server financial systems. Interested in the modelling and web based deployment of systems in the financial domain.

Topics

  • Exploration of attendees use of business process modelling with use cases for in-house commercial systems. Is there agreement on what a business process is?

  • Evolution of business process modelling to commercial web-based systems. The impact of changing user populations and roles, the applicability of use cases to web-based business processes, the challenge of construction and deployment of scaleable web-base business systems over a variety of delivery channels.

  • Adaptation of development styles to construct commercial internet systems. Requirements capture and prototyping user interfaces without users, levering existing enterprise systems with static development processes, user acceptance testing and release.

Benefits

Participants at the Tutorial will learn:

  • An overview of how business process modelling with use cases drives in-house developments of commercial systems.

  • Possible evolutions of business process modelling and development styles for internet based commercial systems.
