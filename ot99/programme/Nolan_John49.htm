The Roots of Failure?

Tuesday 2:30   Think Tank

Trying to get to the root of why the majority of software organizations fail to satisfy

John S. Nolan

The majority of software organizations fail to produce systems which satisfy their customers and produce disgruntled employees along the way. Why? There are so many issues in running a software organization it is often impossible to understand where the roots of the failure lie, especially since you are often too close or part of the problem yourself.

This session is intended to provide an opportunity for participants to put forward and examine the issues facing software organizations within the framework of an alternative "thinking technique" designed to highlight the root issues in problem domains: Edward DeBono's Water Logic.

The main aim here is to achieve a better understanding of the problem not create a solution.

John S. Nolan

I have worked for the last 9 years creating object-oriented software for both commercial and enterprise groups in engineering, finance and elctronic commerce. My continuing interests are in vernacular software architecture, subjective modelling and really useful software tools.

Topics

  • What causes software organizations to fail?
  • Getting to the root of a problem: Edward DeBono's Water Logic

Benefits

The major deliverable of this session will be a description of the root issues facing software organizations which lead to failure, together with an understanding of what contributing factors need to be addressed to avoid them.

Participants will not also learn a useful "thinking technique" (Water Logic) and will also have an opportunity to discuss the issues in software organizations with other professionals.

Level: intermediateAudience: People with experience inimperfect software organizations
