Patterns@Work

Wednesday 2:30   Case study 75 minutes

Using Patterns in Industrial Process Automation

Frank Buschmann

We present in detail a part of a concrete real-world system and how it is designed with patterns: the functional core of a warehouse management system. Step by step we will re-play the process of the system's construction. We discuss the design problems that occur, shortly present the patterns that could help solving these problems, and show how we actually applied the patterns we selected. By this we will see how the design of the system slowly grows and evolves towards the final architecture. We will also see and discuss how patterns are applied in practise and how they help building high-quality software with predictible properties. The case study concludes with a summary of our experiences from several projects in which we applied patterns: what worked, what could be improved, and what did we learn.

Frank Buschmann

Frank Buschmann is software engineer at Siemens Corprate Technology in Munich, Germany. His research interests include Object Technology, Application Frameworks and specifically Patterns. Frank has been involved in several concrete industrial software development projects. Frank is co-author of "Pattern-Oriented Software Architecture -- A System of Patterns"

Benefits

 We want to show how patterns drive the process of building high-quality software, and that applying patterns is not a mechanical task that automatically leeds to success.

Level: advanced Audience: Software Architects/Designers who are familiar with the GoF patterns.

Material

 Either copies of selected transparencies or a short summary of the system in an article style, depending on what you think serves better for the readers.

Delivery

 Workshops, working groups, think-tanks and goldfish-bowls should produce output to record the results of the session

Format

 Generally, a tutorial like style, but trying to include the audience, for example taking suggestions for patterns that might resolve the problem at hand, asking about implications of applying a particular pattern, responding to questions, etc.
