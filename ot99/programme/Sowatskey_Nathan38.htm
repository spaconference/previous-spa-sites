Java, CORBA, and XML

Wednesday 10:15   An architecture for independence

Nathan Sowatskey

The presenter has developed an architecture based on Java, CORBA and XML which is intended to be platform, data and implementation independent.

The experience gained will be described in this session with an overview of developing with Java, an examination CORBA design issues including load-balancing and fault tolerance for servers, CORBA design principles for services and the use of XML as a data description mechanism.

Participants are expected to have a working knowledge of Java and CORBA and an understanding of XML (see other sessions for all three).

Prior reading of the presentation material and preparation of questions will allow everybody to get the most out of the session.

The participants will have the opportunity to learn about the design and implementation of a Java/CORBA/XML production system in detail and the presenter is hoping for (constructive) criticism of the design and implementation decisions made so as to improve the architecture.

Nathan Sowatskey

Nathan has been involved in software development for over 10 years. His commercial background is divided between finance and telecommunications and his technical background is strongly based on OO practices using C++ and Java.

Nathan's financial experience includes stints at Salomon Bros., Merrill Lynch, IBJ, Nomura and JP Morgan where he has worked on both front and back office systems as well as corporate infrastructure and strategic architecture. In the telecommunications field Nathan has worked for BT in the UK on Concert and for Telkom in South Africa on telematics systems and in a leading role for SAIX, the main provider for Internet connectivity in SA.

Audience

 Technical audience. No CORBA prerequisite but a sound understanding of OOand distributed computing is preferred.
