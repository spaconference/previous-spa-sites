Modelling for reusability

Monday 11:45   Tutorial 75 minutes

How to write UML models that can be reused in different contexts

Franco Civello

The tutorial explores ways of developing models of classes or larger software components, which can be reused in different contexts. To achieve this, it is important to represent explicitly what is required by a given class/component.

Franco Civello

Franco has been a lecturer, consultant and researcher in object technology for several years. He has presented the results of his research at national and international conferences, and has written several refereed papers in the area of object modelling. His current research is looking at techniques for representing the valid context of a model, in order to promote model reusability in different contexts (e.g. using a component in different applications).

Topics

    We model components rather than systems Components need to interoperate correctly A component model needs to represent what it needs from other components

    Using the concept of focus or root of a model, it is possible to separate the properties of a component from those that the component requires from its context/environment

Benefits

 The objective is to make the audience aware of the need for and the problems involved in reusing models in different contexts and to suggest a modelling approach in which the requirements for the valid context for a software component are clearly represented and can be checked against the characteristics of the reusing context. Participants will take with them a set of ideas and techniques that they can immediately use in their UML modelling activity, without buying a new tool or using a new language.

Level: intermediateAudience: Analysts, designers and modellers with some knowledge of UMLor equivalent modelling notation.

Material

 The participants will be given the example model and a one sheet introduction to the problem

At the beginning of section 4, a hand-out will be given explaining one possible technique to help produce reusable models

Delivery

 The contributions of the students and the results of the final discussion session will be summarised and made available to participants after the event

Format

 See structure above

Structure

 The session will be divided in five sections:

1) introduction to the objectives and to an example model (15 minutes)

2) participants work on their own, or with their neighbour, to make parts of the model more reusable (15 minutes)

3) participants are invited to share their results (15 minutes)

4) a modelling technique is introduced to make models reusable (20 minutes), and its application to the modelling problem is presented

5) discussion session on the merits of the technique or any other approach contributed by the audience (10 minutes)
