All Dressed Up And Nowhere To Go

Wednesday 10:15   Workshop 170 minutes

A Process for Using Patterns

Russel Winder

Jan Purchase

Patterns are currently an in vogue topic. There are many books on specific patterns and/or collections of patterns, but there seems to be little on how to actually use them in a real project. In many organisations they are used on an ad-hoc basis. This workshop addresses these deficiencies.

Russel Winder

I am Professor of Computing Science at KCL. My research interests center on the development of object-oriented systems, mostly parallel or distributed; this includes technical and human--computer interaction issues in analysis, design and implementation notions. I am involved in the development of various helth informatics systems. For more information see http://www.dcs.kcl.ac.uk/staff/russel/

Jan Purchase

Jan Purchase has been designing and implementing object systems since 1988. He has extensive experience of mentoring, teaching and, above all, using object design and implementation techniques within banks and the public service industries in the UK and the US. Since 1994 he has enjoyed leading roles in financial institutions involving the design and development of extensible and flexible software structures. He is currently designing just such a framework for Bankers Trust's Derivatives Trading Group. His job title has featured the word `architect' for four years.

Topics

  • A brief review of patterns and process model to ensure commonality of domain.

  • A participative exercise in analysis and design using patterns and refactoring patterns.

  • Reflective analysis of the use of patterns in the case study.

Benefits

By attending this workshop attendees will obtain a better understanding of what patterns are and how they can be and should be used in the context of a development process.

Level: intermediateAudience: Practicing software engineers and software engineering researchers interested in patterns.

Material

Notes on the initial presentation. A quick guide to some important patterns. Design Excerise complete with constraints and factors pertinent to pragmatic development. Activity notes.

Delivery

A poster (and later a paper) summarizing the findings of the workshop.

(Optionally) A BOF/soapbox to discuss the findings and our own views.

Format

Participants will be divided into teams of 4-5 with randomized experience of patterns. The centre-peice of the workshop is a design exercise built to make people think about how they use patterns and how this might be improved

Structure

Introductions of People, Warm up exercise (15mins)
Presentation: Review of Patterns and Processes: Level Setting (15mins)
Presentation: What's wrong with the ad-hoc use of Patterns? (10mins)
Design Exercise -- group work (75mins)
Reflection on Exercise -- plenary and presentations (40mins)
Summary and Close (15mins)

During the design exercise people will be free to fetch coffee and other stimulants as required.
