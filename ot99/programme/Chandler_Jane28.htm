Whither away,
Objects, Patterns, Components?

Wednesday 2:30   Goldfish bowl 75 minutes

An open discussion on future developments in object technology and related fields

Jane Chandler

Over the years many areas of object technology have been hailed as the current 'silver bullet'. This goldfish bowl will debate which of these we will still be talking about and using in the next millenium, whether perhaps object technology is now passé and what will be the next hot topic in the OT field.

Jane Chandler

Jane has been involved with object technology since 1989 when she was first introduced to Smalltalk. She is Programme Chair for OT99 and Chair of the BCS OOPS Group.

Jane is a Principal Lecturer in the Department of Information Systems at the University of Portsmouth where she teaches object technology to 160+ students a year. Her research interests include object technology, teaching methods for object technology and Computer Aided Learning (in particular Intelligent Computer Aided Learning).

Topics

  • Current fashions in object technology
  • Future fashions in object technology
  • The reality of object technology for businesses in the next 5 years

Benefits

 An opportunity to crystal ball gaze and debate the future of objects in the company of those in the know

Level: intermediateAudience: Anyone with a view about the future or interested in finding out others' views of the future

Material

 One side A4 overview of the last ten years' hot topics and developments in object technology

Delivery

 Poster

Format

 Usual goldfish bowl style. 5 minutes introductions and explanations of how a goldfish bowl works 5 minutes for initial discussion group statements 60 minutes discussion 5 minutes wrap up

Additionally either myself or a volunteer will note down comments of particular interest on a flip chart, so forming the basis of a poster.

Structure

 An intial, small discussion group of 4 or 5 people, will be set up with the rest of the participants forming an audience. Spare places in the discussion group will be available, and members of the audience may take up these places when they feel they have a contribution to make. Members of the discussion group leave their places when they have had their say, making room for new participants.

The initial discussion group will be formed from people attending OT99 who are confident in this style of setting. They will be primed beforehand and asked to start by making a brief, 60 second statement. I will act as facilitator and have available some additional comments and questions available should the discussion flag.
