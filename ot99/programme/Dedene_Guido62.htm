Experiences in teaching using a strict OO language

Wednesday 10:15   Case Study 75 minutes

Guido Dedene

This case study will describe our experience using a strict OO language to teach programming to students with no programming experience . The introductory Information Systems course at the K.U.Leuven University introduces first-year Economy Students to software development. For many years, this had been a conventional Pascal course. Three years ago, we took the decision to switch to a strict OO-approach, using Eiffel as a replacement for Pascal. Furthermore, the new course stresses the importance of using existing components. Students are not required to write a program from scratch; instead they must demonstrate their ability to complete a system, for which some components are fully or partially developed." The case study will explain the didactical aspects, including the roadmap of examples, the exercise techniques, the didactical infrastructure and the evaluation methods.

We will explain the choice of Eiffel, in comparison with Java, C++ and Smalltalk. Finally, the case study will discuss the student reactions after the first three years. and ask which aspects have been successful, and what might be improved in future

Guido Dedene (guido.dedene@econ.kuleuven.ac.be)

Guido Dedene

Prof. dr. Guido Dedene is Professor in Management Information Systems at the Department of Applied Economics at the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven. His lecturing and research activities concentrate on formal methods for systems development and quantitative systems management techniques. For five years, Guido was also Director of European Services for GUIDE and SHARE Europe, an association of over 2000 mainframe users. Guido Dedene is the author of several journal and conference publications, one of which was granted the IEEE Best Practice paper award.
