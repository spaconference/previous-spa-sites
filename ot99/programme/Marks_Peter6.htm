In Search of a Simpler Way

Tuesday 9:00   Workshop

Peter Marks

Why is our approach to software development so complex? Do our procedures, methodologies and controls really help us to deliver software on time and on budget? Do they help us to deliver better software? Do they help us to meet our clients needs? Do we need more structure to our development process, more formality to our approach, more complexity, or is there a simpler way?

While the mainstream of thought on the dynamics of software development tries to lead us to a world of industrialised software engineering, pragmatism has given rise to an alternative philosophy of JFDI (Just F***ing Do It). Expressed in the ideas of RAD, Extreme Programming and the Programmersí Stone, the underlying view is of software development as a craft.

This workshop seeks to explore this view. It will question the values we hold, and through sharing of ideas and group creativity, will further our understanding of the techniques, philosophy and culture of developing software in a simpler way.

Peter Marks

Peter Marks has been developing software since childhood. Now working as a freelance consultant focusing on software architecture and the dynamics of software development, he is increasingly of the belief that, as software developers, we should seek to regain our childish invention, playful experimentation and imagination.

Topics

  • What values do we hold for software?
  • What values should we set for development?
  • How can we deliver high value software?

Benefits

Participants will gain insight into the craft of software development and develop ideas on improving their own approach.
