Is there a difference betwen Objects and Components?

Monday 11:45   GOLDFISH BOWL - 75 minutes

Ian Graham

This session will explore the issue of whether the recent emphasis on components is justified as something genuinely different from object technology or merely supplier-motivated hype. Participants will be invited to air their opinions and debate this and the following auxiliary questions:

  • What exactly is the difference, if any, between a component and an object?
  • Do components delivered as binaries which cannot be modified by inheritance throw away most of the advantages of object-oriented programming?
  • What is the ideal granularity of components, if there is one?
  • What effect does the component viewpoint have on language design and language selection?
  • Are IDL semantics rich enough to describe 'business object' components?
  • What are the implications for object-oriented methods?
This session will appeal to all developers working in environments that include the possibility of component-based development using CORBA, Active X, Java, C++, Eiffel, or similar. Managers making strategic or product purchasing decisions will also benefit from the discussion.

Ian Graham (grahami@compuserve.com)

Ian Graham is an internationally recognized business process modelling and object technology consultant. He is a widely published authority on advanced information technology with over 20 years experience as an IT practitioner and industry consultant. Ian is the author or editor of nine books including "Migrating to Object Technology" and "Requirements Engineering - An object-oriented approach" (Addison-Wesley) which describe his object-oriented software engineering method, SOMA, in detail and a regular contributor to journals. He is a popular and experienced presenter and is regularly invited to speak at conferences around the world. He is Principal Consultant for Ian Graham Associates Ltd. and Industrial Professor of Requirements Engineering at De Montfort University.

Ian has degrees in Mathematics from Hull and London Universities and is a Fellow of the British Computer Society and a Chartered Engineer. He resides in London, England and may be contacted on: grahami@compuserve.com
