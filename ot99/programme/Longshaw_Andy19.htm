EJB vs MTS

Wednesday 2:30   Tutorial 75 minutes

The destiny of server-side components - a blow-by-blow comparison of the major contenders

Andy Longshaw

Mike Tricker

As the component market has matured, attention has turned to use of server-side components. Particular attention has been focused on providing transactional environments for business components. The two main proposed solutions in this area are Sun's Enterprise JavaBeans (EJB) and Microsoft's Transaction Server (MTS). In addition to transactions, these environments address important issues such as object pooling and thread synchronisation.

This tutorial will examine the requirements for a server-side component infrastructure, such as object/resource pooling, locating services, object lifecycle, state management, portability and interoperability. The two environments will then be examined to identify strengths and weaknesses in each area. Also, development choices and deployment issues in each environment will be examined.

Benefits

 Participants will gain the knowledge to appreciate the issues relating to server-side components which can be used when judging products and services in this space. Participants who will be implementing this type of system will also be able to judge which architecture is most suited to their requirements.
Level: intermediate Audience: technical architects, technical managers, server-side developers

Material

 Handouts of the foils will be delivered. After the session, the framework generated will be written up and made available.

Delivery

 No deliverables except for material described above.

Format

 The format will be part instructional delivery about the capabilities and structure of EJB and MTS/COM+ and part workshop to create a server-side component framework and discuss how the two technologies fit into it.

Structure

    After the introduction and outline of the session, the attendees will form teams to generate an idealised framework for server-side components. This will define, amongst other things, the environment and services provided for such components, ease of deployment and ongoing management.

    Once the framework is developed, two short sessions will outline the primary structure and capabilities of EJB and MTS/COM+. This should provide ammunition for those unfamiliar with one or both to use when comparing them to the framework.

    Finally, a group discussion will use the framework as a benchmark against which to compare both EJB and MTS/COM+.
