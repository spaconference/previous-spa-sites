Pleading against message passing

Why message passing is inadequate for object-oriented analysis

Monique Snoeck

Guido Dedene

Object-orientation started with programming languages. OO Design and analysis methods have taken over the same ideas virtually unchanged. However, as this tutorial will show, message passing is far too implementation-specific to be helpful for OO analysis. Instead, we propose a new interaction mechanism, at a higher level of abstraction. In this tutorial, we shall teach this new form of interaction and link it to message passing. Delegates will learn the advantages of this new form of interaction by developing a simple example. The transition to design will show the possible implementation options.

Monique Snoeck

Dr. Monique Snoeck is researcher at the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven and invited lecturer at the Université Catholique de Louvain. She has a two years practical experience in the banking sector and over ten years research experience in the field of object-oriented analysis and formal methods. She is one of the main researchers behind the MERODE method and the main author of the MERODE book on object-oriented enterprise modelling.

Guido Dedene

Prof. dr. Guido Dedene is Professor in Management Information Systems at the Department of Applied Economics at the Katholieke Universiteit Leuven. His lecturing and research activities concentrate on formal methods for systems development and quantitative systems management techniques. For five years, Guido was also Director of European Services for GUIDE and SHARE Europe, an association of over 2000 mainframe users. Guido Dedene is the author of several journal and conference publications, one of which was granted the IEEE Best Practice paper award.

Benefits

 You will learn about a new interaction mechanism for object-oriented analysis. You will see how this concept gives you complete freedom in the choice of implementation environment, being object-oriented of not.

Session: Tutorial 75 minutes Level: basic Audience: Anybody interested in innovative approaches to OO analysis, anybody wishing to know how to match OO with legacy IT.

Material

 We will design a poster that summarises our arguments against message passing for object-oriented analysis and promote the alternative interaction mechanism.

Delivery
