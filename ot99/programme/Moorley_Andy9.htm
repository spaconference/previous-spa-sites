Architects are from Mars, Developers are from Venus?

Wednesday 10:15   Workshop 170 minutes

A Practical Guide for Improving Communication and Understanding between architects and developers.

Andy Moorley

Are architects and developers just the same sort of people but on different parts of their career path or are they fundamentally different types of people. In his book, Dr. John Gray suggests that men and woman are from "different planets" and that having understood this, more successful relationships can be built. In this session we will consider whether a similar situation exits between those in the team who are "architects" and those who are "developers".

Andy Moorley

Andy works for JP Morgan (London) where he is the "Architect" for an OO Derivatives Risk Management system based on Smalltalk, ODBMS and ORB technology. He has both architectural and development roles in this project.

moorley_andy@jpmorgan.com

Topics

  • Examine Characteristics and distinguishing features of developers/architects
  • Review of personal experiences to create problem scenarios
  • Creation of organisational and process patterns to improve Architect/Developer communication

Benefits

Let's be honest - most problems in software engineering come about because of poor communication. In this session we will explore the communication problems that can occur between those who have responsibilities for the architecture of a project and those responsible for the "coal-face" development. By the end of the session we aim to have

  • Understood whether there are real differences between developers and architects
  • Gained an understanding of the likely areas of communication difficulties
  • Created some process or organisational patterns (or at least to have looked an some existing ones) to help improve the communication between these two groups

    Level: AllAudience: Are you a developer who wishes the project architect would "get real"? Are you an architect who wishes the developers would have a vision that went beyond the next coding deadline? Are you a manager who spends time trying to broker the peace between these two groups? If the answer to any of these is yes, the chances are that you need to be here!

    Format

    		 Much of this workshop is spent in various small groups. However, they will work in different ways at different points as laid out below:

    Architect/Developer profiles - In groups of between 3 and 6, we will attempt to analysis the personality of real and stereotypical architects and developers. Three main techniques will be available to the groups. For those people who would count themselves clearly in (and happy in) one role or the other, they can use one of the standard personality profile tests. Another approach for people who have fulfilled or experienced both roles, then it it suggested they role-play one of the stereotypes - and then either complete one of the charts or simply create lists of what the like-dislike about each other. A third (and somewhat more detached) approach will be allowed whereby an initial list of descriptions (such as "forceful", "attention to detail", "abstract thinker") where will be provide and they can indicate which role might be described as this. The list should be extended by the group.

    TThe Group Therapy session, although mainly there to provide material for later in the workshop, is actually a valuable exercise in its own right. People will be encourage to share in an open and non-condemnatory atmosphere their experiences. Two models of operation will be offered (to suite different personalities in the group). One model requires groups of there people. Each person will in turn take the role of "priest" (i.e. active listener), "confessor" and "scribe". Each person will get about 6-7 minutes in each role. The second model can accommodate groups of 3-5 and is based on the "Alcoholics Anonymous rules" - (e.g. no interrupting, no judgement etc). Again each contributor would be time limited.

    When it comes to the main group work two choices will be offered. Choice one is to take one of the problems shared by the group (either one which was resolved or one not) and investigate it further. Initially this will focus on trying to understand the forces, thinking about exactly what the problem was/is - and then considering the solution(s) and any new problems created. This hopefully will then lead onto expression in the problem/forces/ solution/resulting context form of a proto-pattern. If we find common themes coming up - then may be we have a new pattern which we can pass back into the Organisation Patterns work.

    Choice two would be more suited to people who have less direct experience to offer or just find this approach more useful. In this case, a group will be given a few (5ish) of the existing Organisational Patterns and will be encouraged to consider how these might help with the problems from our shared list.

    Structure

    (5) Intro

    (15) Group work - Using role-play and personality profile charts we will brainstorm characteristics/personality profiles of architects and developers

    (10) Quick feedback from group work

    (10) Plenary discussion - Are they from the same planet?

    (30) Group Therapy - Tell our war stories and from these identify three problems which we might want

    to build solutions for.

    (15) Each group presents quick problem summaries and an indication of whether they had a happy ending. Combine to form a central list.

    [Break]

    (5) Explain Group work

    (60) Group work - Examining problems and solutions/Testing existing patterns/creating new patterns

    (15) Poster bazaar - Each group has summarised their work and thoughts and all participants then browse the results

    (10) Plenary discussion/wrap up
