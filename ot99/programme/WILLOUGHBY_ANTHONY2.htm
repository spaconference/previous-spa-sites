Business modelling with UML.

Tuesday 2:30   Workshop 150 minutes

How to use the techniques in the UML to analyse and document business processes.

ANTHONY WILLOUGHBY

The main aim of the UML is to provide a set of techniques for modelling software in an object oriented manner. It has been claimed, however, that these techniques could be applied to almost any domain. Some of the techniques (e.g. business process extensions to use cases) have been enhanced to make them more suitable for the business domain. This is particularly relevant to describing processes which make use of software systems as well as more human-oriented tasks.

The tutorial part will describe the main techniques available in the UML, with examples of their use. One or two non-UML techniques will be described briefly for comparison. The utility of the domain object model will be emphasised as a way of providing rigour. Finally the relationship between business and system models, for business processes which incorporate software systems, will be explored.

The workshop part will consist of the participants dividing into groups and analysing a simple and well understood process using technique(s) of their choosing. Selected groups will then present their findings, enabling participants to actively compare the effectiveness of the approaches used.

ANTHONY WILLOUGHBY

Anthony Willoughby is an independent consultant working on object technology and component-based development, especially in relation to analysis and design. He also delivers training courses on behalf of Select, Rational and his own company. He has been object oriented for about 10 years.

Topics

  • Tutorial on techniques for business modelling - activity diagrams, use case models with business process extensions, domain object models, plus one or two non-UML methods for comparison.

  • Interaction between business models and software system models.

  • Hands-on modelling using chosen techniques.

Benefits

 Participants will gain an understanding of the different techniques available in the UML for business modelling, an appreciation of their strengths and weaknesses, and will distinguish clearly between business modelling and software system modelling.

Level: intermediate Audience: Business modellers not familiar with UML; System analysts; Software professionals wishing to learn about business modelling.

Material

A document describing each of the modelling techniques which will be introduced in the session.

Delivery

 Any output produced by the groups could be delivered if of adequate legibility.

Format

Semi-formal lecture during first part. As mentor/helper during mini-workshop part (I have given many training courses with this type of format).

Structure

    1 Tutorial on techniques for business modelling - 30 minutes.

    2 Introduction to example problem - 5 mins

    3 Work on problem in groups of ~ 4 20 mins

    4 Selected groups present their solutions on posters to other participants

  • 10 minutes.

    5 General discussion and wind up by me. No doubt those experienced in business modelling may have their own comments to make at this stage. 10 minutes

    Total 75 mins.
