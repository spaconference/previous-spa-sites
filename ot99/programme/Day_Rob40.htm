Getting IT wrong

Tuesday 4:00   Play 75 minutes

An OO Tragedy

Rob Day

Alan Cameron Wills

Nicolas Chagrin

Acme Elephant Dentistry has expanded from its beginnings in rural Bedfordshire to many branches throughout Europe and beyond. Each national division is started as a copy of the parent, with its own copy of business procedures and software. Now the company want to go multinational so that they can deal with multinational clients and work across national borders.

But with the passage of time, local business practices and software in each national branch have diverged. They no longer talk the same language and their software can't talk to each other. They have tried to set up a central "software factory", but all sorts of wrangles have resulted. A succession of managers and have failed to resolve the situation. As the competition catches up, the senior management see billions of dollars wasted.

Ultimately, a workable migration plan is found, but not without considerable pain.

This session dramatises the experience of a real company known to the presenters.

Rob Day

Rob Day is a software project management consultant based in England, specialising in the management, estimation and metrics for large OO developments. Rob has been involved in structured and object-oriented projects in the various roles of analyst, project manager, reviewer, consultant, and instructor since 1985. He has consulted and taught in the United States, France, Belgium and Britain. Rob has developed courses in OO project management for various training organisations and is accredited to teach Shlaer-Mellor, OMT and UML OOA/D courses. Rob's OO project management experiences range across a considerable variety of applications including defence, telecomms, aerospace, banking and insurance. Rob has been a business development manager and much of Rob's more recent work has focused on linking IT into the business, particularly in the context of major business change.

Alan Cameron Wills

Alan Cameron Wills is a consultant in object and component technology with clients in a variety of fields including finance, telecoms and embedded systems. He is technical director of TriReme.

Nicolas Chagrin

Nicolas Chagrin is a successful Professional Actor with over thirty years' experience in Theatre, Films, and TV (House of Elliot, Darling Buds of May, The Knock, etc) who also works in the business arena at all levels and uses skills and techniques from the performing arts to enable people to make more dynamic and effective presentations.
