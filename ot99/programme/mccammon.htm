Enabling Enterprise Components

Keiron McCammon

Versant LTD

Object-Oriented lanaguages have failed to deliver the wholesale re-use that once they promised. Few have managed to achieve significant success above the implementation of class libraries. Why? The answer generally cited: "it's too damn difficult". Delivering re-use across projects at the object level is hard. It requires the wholesale use of common infrastructure capabilities, this infrastructure provides the bed upon which developers can lay their own applications, assured that they will be able to interoperate with others. Re-use is now defined at a much coarser level of granularity than the object, now its defined the component level (although lets not be fooled , O-O languages and principles are of course the underpinnings of the component).

This talk aims to take a look at what it takes to build and deliver enterprise components, highlighting the role of CORBA, Enterprise Java Beans and DCOM, but focusing on the issue of middle-tier persistence and the role of Versant in solving this problem.

Component development requires a re-think of the role of the database, no longer is the database king, and for complex components existing database technologies don't offer the capabilities to handle complex data and complex data relationships in a performant manner. Components need a reliable, highly performant middle-tier persistence mechanism to support the business processing being performed there. Versant as a proven ODBMS demonstrably provides the capability to build highly scalable, performant components that are unconstrained by the limitations of older technologies. But middle-tier persistence cannot exist in a vacuum, it must be possible to coexist with exiting operational data and database solutions. To this end Versant's universal data access capability aims to deliver a unified object view of operational data to the middle-tier, managing the mapping to and from the middle-tier and the operational systems as an administrative function.

By the end of this talk you will hopefully have gained an insight into the role of components and importantly the need to re-think the role played by a database in the middle-tier, as Albert Einstein once said:

"YOU DON'T SOLVE PROBLEMS WITH THE SAME KIND OF THINKING THAT CREATED THEM"
