Developing softer software.

Monday 11:45   Case study, with interactive demo and buzz-groups.

Experience report on incremental development of a framework supporting organizational inquiry and learning.

Martine Devos

In this session I report on experience with the development of a framework and applications for administration and on a supporting method for incremental development and organizational learning. The framework helps us to develop applications iterative, interactive and incrementally and allows us to focus on the process of problem appreciation by both end-user and developer. The framework is used to develop applications, which share a common business logic and require database, electronic documents, workflow and Internet functionality. The framework allows to build increasingly complete specifications of applications that can be executed at once. The resulting system can easily be adapted to changing business needs. The user can develop new applications through modeling and configuration, rather than through coding.

The framework is based on a repository on two levels. First, it consists of a set of tools for managing a repository of documents, data and processes, including their history and current status. With these tools users select applications, enter and view data, query the repository, access thesaurus and electronic documents, workflow processes and task assignments. The repository drives the framework behavior. We store knowledge of the business model, none of which hard coded. Tools consult the repository at runtime. End-users can change the system's behavior using high-level tools to change the business model. This way, we separate the organization's business logic from the application functionality and build softer software, resilient to change.

Since we can afford users to change their mind, inspired by the hands-on learning experience of the technology and the new opportunities it offers, we use methods and techniques from soft system thinking to encourage users to reflect on their applications and to envision better ways of working, even after initial delivery. To incorporate the feedback we adapt both framework and the applications. Support of a bootstrapping process was an important secondary design goal: administration and configuration tools are now expressed in terms of the framework.

Martine Devos

Martine Devos recently joined EDS as Business Planning Consultant. She was director of the IS-department at Argo since 1992 and before has worked as teacher, as technical consultant and project leader to the Belgian Minister of Education and the Civil Service. As IS manager she introduced objects and she initiated, and coordinated the development of a framework and several applications using it. Her main focus is on the use of IS - and "softer software" - to support change programs and learning. Special interests are framework development, the use of patterns in organization, the human side of IS and facilitating workshops. She participated in, and organized, several workshops and panels on System Envisioning, the use of creativity in requirements developing. She is co-chair of EuroPlop'99.
