Frameworks for business objects in large trading systems

Martin Coen, Neil Lancaster, Roy MacLean

TCAM

This case study involves the use of an object-oriented framework, FLEX, as the basis for development of systems for one of the world's largest trading exchanges. The framework encapsulates functionality as business objects, which are implemented in Eiffel, and are distributed using the DAIS Object Request Broker.

The session illustrate how a variety of technologies and design ideas have been brought together to address the goals of flexibility and scalability. It examines the way in which ORBs are used in large-scale systems, and offers views on business objects, dynamic typing, design patterns, Eiffel, and the development process.

The session is aimed at those with an interest in applying object technology to the development of large-scale distributed systems. Although the business domain is Exchange Trading, the technologies and design ideas are of wide applicability: participants do not need knowledge of the financial sector.

Particular emphasis is placed on the design alternatives, the choices made, and the reasons underlying those choices. Input from the audience will be encouraged as the alternatives are discussed.

Key topics

Business context and requirements

Design goals: scalability, flexibility, reuse

Large-scale design: how to subdivide a system; business objects; how to build one; components and classes; how to store one; function versus data; dynamic typing and type-objects

Distribution: getting the granularity right; interface objects; to ORB or not to ORB?

Class design and language: why Eiffel; design patterns applied or discovered; using assertions

Methodology: the development process; libraries versus systems versus applications; what is reusable?

Martin Coen is Director of the Exchange Systems Group in TCAM. In four and a half years at TCAM, Martin has worked on a wide range of financial systems, both object-oriented and non-object-oriented.

Neil Lancaster has also been with TCAM for over four years, working on a variety of systems, particularly in the area of distribution. Neil is responsible for the infrastructure area of the FLEX framework, including the DAIS ORB.

Roy MacLean joined TCAM in 1996 from Logica. Roy is responsible for the development of business applications using the FLEX framework.
