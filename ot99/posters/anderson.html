Pattern languages for capturing excellence

Bruce Anderson, Rory Woodward

IBM Object Technology Practice

There are people and teams who build excellent solutions - small or large, local or distributed, one-off or product-line.

An important recent direction in application development is to accept that we can learn from, and build on, this excellence.

One increasingly popular way to capture this learning is with Design Patterns, prototypical and reusable segments of solutions. Another way is through pattern languages, networks of small specific solution patterns, where the generality comes from choosing patterns for the current problem context.

Although there are some published pattern languages, in general details are not made public, as they are often specific to the context of the work, and also because they represent valuable intellectual property.

Key topics

What are pattern languages

How do pattern languages differ from design patterns

Examples of pattern languages that we can learn from

Areas where pattern languages can be used productively

How can teams use and create pattern languages for business advantage
