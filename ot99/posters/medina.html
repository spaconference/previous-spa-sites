Object architectures and internet applications

Rolando Medina

Unipart Information Technology

Despite the unparalleled growth in use of the internet that has taken place over the last three years, internet technology has some shortcomings in terms of meeting today's demands for business functionality. This case study shows how a business internet-based application can be developed using object technology and a Java-based middle-tier. The result is robust and flexible applications capable of meeting the challenges of future changes in technology and business requirements.

This session compares alternative solutions in terms of investment, flexibility, performance, portability, scalability, interoperability, and resilience. It also discusses gains that can be obtained in terms of cost, rapid application development, and rapid application delivery.

This case study demonstrates that object technology (including CORBA) applied to internet solutions has the potential to provide industrial-strength applications at lower cost and risk, increased flexibility (applications can be developed over intranet, extranet, and internet), and with faster development and delivery.

Key topics

  • Combining internet and object technologies
  • Comparison of different approaches
  • Costs and benefits
  • Electronic commerce issues
  • Applets versus HTML-based clients
  • JavaBeans / OLE components and CORBA
  • Java on clients and Java on servers: key issues
  • Access to CORBA-based servers that interface to legacy applications
  • IBM Network Computing Framework: Notes, Domino, Java, IIOP

Rolando Medina is a Senior Technical Consultant with Unipart Information Technology (part of the Unipart group of Companies).

Rolando is an expert in software development and for the past five years has been involved in the development of distributed systems using object technology.
