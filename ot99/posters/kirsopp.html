Using measurement to control object-oriented projects

Colin Kirsopp, Steve Webster

Bournemouth University

Tom DeMarco has suggested that 'you can't control what you can't measure'. In object-oriented systems it is not always that easy to know what to measure but object-oriented projects still have to be controlled. This tutorial summarises the material discussed and developed in the working group of the same name (being held on Sunday 5 April), which examines the relationship between the object-oriented development process and software measurement.

It is all too common for research in this area to consider metrics in isolation from the process within which measurement is made. From an industrial viewpoint measurement often appears as an academic topic unrelated to the problems and requirements of software production in the real world. Metrics such as those from Chidamber & Kemerer have been proposed in theory and used in practice, but never in a really convincing fashion.

Academia and industry need to collaborate to produce sound measurement research that is transferred into industrial practice.

Key topics

Measuring software products and processes

- State of the art

- Academic research versus commercial needs

- Integrating measurement and development

Using measurement to control software production

- Current inadequacies

- New research into measurement-based process control

- Improvements for the future

Colin Kirsopp holds a masters degree in software engineering and is currently a researcher at Bournemouth University. His research interests include object-oriented metrics, measurement and process integration, software architectures, and automated design analysis. Colin was awarded the 1996 BCS OOPS Specialist Group scholarship for the advancement of object technology in the UK.

Steve Webster is a senior lecturer in software engineering at Bournemouth University with a research interest in object-oriented design methods. He teaches object-oriented design to graduate and post-graduate students and consults for industrial companies that are adopting design methods and CASE tools.
