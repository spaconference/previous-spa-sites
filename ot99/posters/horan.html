Requirements for collaborative development environments

Bernard Horan

Sun Microsystems Laboratories

bernard.horan@sun.com


The combination of intranet, internet, and object technology offers a promise of 'write once, run anywhere' applications. But how do we get to that promise from the current state of 'write here, crash there' applications?

This session suggests that what will help us fulfil that promise are object-oriented development environments that recognise the importance of collaboration among the various stakeholders in the software development process.

The think-tank provides a forum for sketching out a vision of such a development environment. The objective of the think-tank is simple: to discover what features such an environment requires, and to build a vision of a collaborative development environment.

Key topics

  • Synchronous versus asynchronous working
  • Virtual presence
  • Debugging: in-process or out-process?
  • Audio and video features
  • Assigning capabilities to users
  • Configuration management
