How software learns

Peter Marks Independent peter@digitauk.demon.co.uk

Tim Mackinnon OTI UK Ltd timm@oti.com

In his book and TV series How buildings learn, Steward Brand examines how buildings are changed and developed after they are built. He identifies different paths of change and proposes that buildings adapt best when their design is considered in layers. He suggests that time creates the greatest buildings, but only if their architecture allows them to learn.

There are many lessons to be learned here, not just by architects of buildings, but by architects of software.

This workshop investigates the parallels between architecture in buildings and in software and, by drawing analogies from Brand's work, looks at how we might design software that learns well.

Peter Marks is a software architect working as an independent consultant in the financial information and educational software industries. He has been involved with object technology for over ten years and is currently interested in the application of concepts, principles, and approaches from other design disciplines to software development.

Tim Mackinnon is a software developer at OTI UK limited. He has been working with objects for seven years and his interests include developing software in the team environment and software architecture.
