Design by translation

Campbell McCausland

Project Technology International

Translation is the generic term for the (typically automated but at least tightly defined) conversion of analysis work products through design to code. Translation is set to be one of the hot topics for the next decade. During a recent public debate between translation proponent Stephen Mellor and translation opponent Grady Booch at OOPSLA 96, Booch was obliged to accept the reality of translation.

What are the benefits of translation? What is wrong with elaboration anyway? Why has translation not been possible before? What role do objects play in translation? Do patterns fit into the picture? Are there any down-sides to translation? How do I get started? This workshop will answer these, and other, questions. It identifies and reports on the maturity features needed for a method to support automated translation.
