The end of the age of miracles

Richard Dué

Thomsen Dué and Associates

This session explores a systems development approach that allows end users, analysts, and designers to independently develop integrated systems. The use of Proxy and Broker patterns are proposed in a new approach to the project management and architecture of object technology application systems.

The tutorial also investigates the use of these patterns with the 'software piranha' and 'duck' design philosophies. The software piranha philosophy uses event-driven design to stimulate the behaviours and responsibilities of independent objects. The duck philosophy places all interpretation of behaviour into separate observing objects. The goal is the elimination of the 'miracles' that are required to transform analysis to design and design to code in traditional software engineering approaches.

Ed Yourdon calls 'The end of the age of miracles; approach 'novel and intriguing', but wonders how it will fare in the market-place over the next few years. The prime objective of this session is to critique and enhance the approach.

Key topics

The end of the age of miracles approach

Impact on analysis, design, and project organisation

The 'piranha' and 'duck' design philosophies

Review of participants' experience with object technology application projects

Enhancements through additional development patterns and philosophies

Directions for further research and development

Richard Dué has 33 years' IT experience as a programmer, analyst, manager, consultant, university professor, author, and expert witness. He is a frequent speaker at many international conferences including OOPSLA, ECOOP, CAiSE, TOOLS, and Software Development. His object technology articles have appeared in Object Magazine, Database Programming and Design, American Programmer, Datamation, and Object Expert. Since 1989, he has been the Information Economics columnist for the journal, Information Systems Management. Richard is a member of the OPEN methodology consortium.
