Object discovery: a process for developing applications

Tony Simons

University of Sheffield

Do you ever get the impression that object-oriented methods offer little direction and rely too much on the intuition and expertise of the developer team? Have you experienced failure in applying a so-called 'method', only to find that it was merely a set of notations? Do you need a directed approach to developing object-oriented software that has the same strength as SSADM for traditional software?

The Discovery method is designed to overcome these difficulties. In Discovery, activities and techniques are selected and sequenced according to their ability to involve the client, focus the mind of the developer, provide visual feedback, facilitate discovery and invention of objects, and support systematic cross-checking. The method asks questions like: Why was this technique developed? What aspect does it accentuate? How does it direct our attention? What can we measure, evaluate, and cross-check? How can we incorporate patterns, frameworks, and libraries? It provides an object-oriented process that can be applied immediately to medium-sized projects. Discovery is UML and OPEN compliant.

This session shows how to use the method to sequence and link particular techniques chosen for their effectiveness in focusing the mind of the developer-team on the next development stage.

Tony Simons is chairman of the OPEN Consortium's Working Group on Technical Process, and a co-author of the forthcoming book The OPEN toolbox of techniques (Addison-Wesley). He teaches object-oriented programming (C++, Eiffel, Smalltalk) and analysis and design (the Discovery method) in the Department of Computer Science, University of Sheffield. He has presented tutorials at OOPSLA and ECOOP, and served on the OOPSLA 95 programme committee.
