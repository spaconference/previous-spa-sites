Is ORB technology ready for the big time?

Colin Crist

APR Consulting

colin.crist@acm.org


At a birds-of-a-feather session during last year's Object Technology conference, experiences of using object request brokers were elicited. The results of this discussion were not promising for proponents of ORB technology: only a handful of participants believed that the technology was mature enough to support large distributed applications in business, and these with reservations. This session provides a follow-up to last year's discussion in the form of a structured forum for participants to explain and discuss their use of ORBs.

The session addresses the choice, introduction, use, and management of ORB technology, with participants sharing their experience of addressing these issues. The session will provide an assessment of the maturity of ORB technology.

Who should attend?

Anyone using ORB technology to implement distributed object systems, evaluating ORB products or considering their use.

Objectives

  • Present an opportunity for asking and answering questions surrounding the choice, introduction, use and management of ORB technology
  • Share experience of addressing these issues
  • Provide an indication of the perceived maturity of ORB technology

Structure of the session

The session will be run as a goldfish-bowl with wizards. These wizards are invited contributors (maximum 4) with particularly strong experience or views: their role is to initiate and provoke the discussion.

Participants at the session will be required to write down at least one question each on a form provided, and will be asked to add any answers either as they arise or at the end of the session. The completed sheets will serve as a measure of the effectiveness of the session (was your question asked? was it answered?) and will be collected at the end to form part of the session output

An introduction will present the rules of play. During the session normal goldfish-bowl rules will apply (the wizard's have a wild-card which allows them to eject a participant from the table if they need to). A non-participating helper will act as scribe to record salient points in the discussion. The session will be rounded up with summaries from the wizards and presenters, completion of the question/answer forms, reflection on the process and a vote on the session's leading question.

Output

As part of the session process, participants will record their own principal questions and note answers which arise. These will be collected and summarised for presentation on a poster, along with any other significant findings, and will be written up for publication.
