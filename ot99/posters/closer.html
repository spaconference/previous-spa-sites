By the end of the conference participants are likely to be brim-full with new information that requires careful consideration and arrangement. This session is to help participants round off and complete their time at Object Technology 98. Participants will meet in their Home Groups to review their experiences and their learning, and to make plans for carrying this learning into their working lives.

An important part of this closing plenary will be interaction between the Home Groups to discuss results with others and create a broader perspective on the conference and the world of object technology.

David Harvey has over a decade of experience in designing, building, and managing the development of software. He is currently implementing an advanced software architecture for derivatives trading for a City and London institution. As a consultant he has helped organisations make effective use of object technology, and has delivered introductory and advanced training courses in C++ and object-oriented design.

Peter Marks is a software architect working as an independent consultant in the financial information and educational software industries. He has been involved with object technology for over ten years and is currently interested in the application of concepts, principles, and approaches from other design disciplines to software development.

Jane Chandler is a Principal Lecturer in the Department of

Information Systems at the University of Portsmouth where she is

responsible for staff research development and the Computers in

Education Research Group. She has been using and teaching object-oriented techniques (including Smalltalk and Responsibility Driven Design) since 1989.

Alan Cameron Wills has worked as a consultant in object technology since 1990, specialising in modelling, analysis, and design. He is the co-author of Objects, frameworks and components with Catalysis, a new book in the UML series. He has worked with clients in a wide variety of application areas and is a director of Trireme, the specialist training and software development consultancy.
