Modelling concurrent business processes

Ian Graham

Chase Manhattan Bank

Peter Llewelyn Jones

Bezant Object Technology

Popular approaches to object modelling, such as UML, use sequence and activity diagrams to model the dynamics of systems. It is evident that a similar approach could be applied to modelling business processes in order to gain a better understanding of requirements and improve those same processes. However, the semantics of the UML sequence diagrams and the underlying metamodel of objects is quite inadequate for this purpose.

This tutorial shows how this situation is remedied by the stronger semantics of SOMA, wherein sequence diagrams do support branching, rendezvous, and concurrency and where activity diagrams (use cases) are generated automatically leading to testable, executable specifications. The arguments are illustrated using a case study based on a large modelling project.

Ian Graham is an internationally recognised business process re-engineering and object technology consultant with over 20 years' experience as an IT practitioner. Ian is author/editor of nine books including Migrating to object technology (Addison-Wesley) which describes his object-oriented method, SOMA, in detail. Currently he is Manager of Development Methods with Chase Manhattan Bank.

Peter Llewelyn Jones is Chairman of Bezant Object Technology and the author of several successful software products, including the SOMATiK tool that supports the SOMA approach, and two books. His latest work Requirements engineering (Addison-Wesley) is co-authored with Ian Graham.
