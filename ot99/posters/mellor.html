Whither methods?

Stephen Mellor

Project Technology Inc

People do not want methods. They want solutions.

Software system construction today is treated as a craft. Time-to-market requirements, the sheer volume of software to be written, and the need for better quality spell the end of the days of designers as artisans. Systems have to be engineered in very different ways.

This keynote session explores a world in which software construction is automated to a large degree; where software is an asset not an expense; and where creativity is focused on problem abstraction.

In this world, software engineers will take on three roles - analyst, architect, and toolsmith - and methods will focus on how to build factories that make software, rather than writing software 'by hand'.

The session outlines the basic technologies that go together to support plug-and-play software: rigorous notations, automated processes, translation, and software-platform independent descriptions of architecture. This technology remains a dream to many practitioners, but the technology to support this vision is here and maturing.

Key topics

Where we lose time and quality in design today

What we need to automate software construction

Application-independent software architectures

Rigorous notations, automated processes, and translation

Stephen Mellor is best known for his contribution to the development of object-oriented analysis and the Shlaer-Mellor Method. He is currently Senior Vice President of Project Technology, Inc., where he serves as co-researcher, with Sally Shlaer, for Project Technology's extensions and applications of the Shlaer-Mellor Method. Mr Mellor and Ms Shlaer are currently working on their third book Shlaer-Mellor Method: recursive design. Mr Mellor is also a member of the editorial board for IEEE Software.
