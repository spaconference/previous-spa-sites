Open interfaces: improving component reusability

Kevin Rutherford

Independent

There is a growing consensus that the unit of reuse in software development is not the single object but is the component, a group of objects working together to provide a service. The principal type of relationship between components in a model of a system is that of dependency; and a component that is dependent on too many others, or on others that are inappropriate in some way, has low reusability.

This session demonstrates the utility of open interfaces, abstractions that permit components to be made less dependent (and hence more reusable). Open interfaces help the architect or designer to apply Meyer's open-closed principle to full effect, achieving more adaptable solutions.

The session offers a systematic view from the architect's perspective, showing how complex systems can be made adaptable by improving the reusability of their component parts.

Participants will gain an enhanced understanding of the usefulness of component-based modelling, and will possess a simple technique for improving component reusability and de coupling large systems.
